Adhiṭṭhāna of Supplementary Robes

A bhikkhu may only make adhiṭṭhāna of one robe of each type, namely saṅghāṭi, uttarāsaṅga, and antaravāsaka, and not two or three of the same type. Therefore, those who wish to use more robes should make adhiṭṭhāna of them as supplementary robes (parikkhāracola). When making adhiṭṭhāna of a single robe, you should say “imaṃ cīvaraṃ parikkhāra coḷaṃ adhiṭṭhāmi” three times and make adhiṭṭhāna. If there are more than one robe, gather them all together and you should say “imāni cīvarāni parikkhāra coḷāni adhiṭṭhāmi”. When making adhiṭṭhāna of robes that are outside your reach, you should say “etaṃ cīvaraṃ parikkhāra coḷaṃ adhiṭṭhāmi” or “etāni cīvarāni parikkhāra coḷāni adhiṭṭhāmi”.

“Niṭṭhitacīvarasmiṃ pana bhikkhunā ubbhatasmiṃ kaṭhine, ekarattaṃ pi ce bhikkhu ticīvarena vippavaseyya aññatra bhikkhu sammutiyā. Nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ”

For bhikkhus who find it difficult to carry and use the three robes wherever they go, so as not to incur an offense by the training rule stated above, there is no fault in not making adhiṭṭhāna of any of them by the names saṅghāṭi, uttarāsaṅga, or antaravāsaka, but rather making adhiṭṭhāna of all of them solely by the name of supplementary robes (parikkhāracola). Currently, many bhikkhus use robes in this manner.

Excluding cloths that are requisites for the dwelling, such as pillowcases and bedsheets, if any type of cloth that is not smaller than a cubit in length and a span in width is kept for more than ten days out of season without being made adhiṭṭhāna of or vikappana, it incurs a Nissaggiya Pācittiya offense. Therefore, one should accustom oneself to making adhiṭṭhāna of or vikappana of all pouches, handkerchiefs, shoulder-cloths, and any other pieces of cloth received, as ‘supplementary robes’ (parikkhāracola).
