Spreading the Kathina with a Pre-made Robe

Because it was said: “Na aññat‍ra pañcakena vā atireka pañcakena vā tadaheva sañchinnena samaṇḍalikatena at‍thataṃ hoti kaṭhinaṃ”, the Kathina is not spread if it is done with a robe that is not cut into five or more parts on that very day the cloth was received, or if it is not made with the great circles and half-circles, or if it is uncut, or cut into only two or three parts.

Many people hold that 'spreading the Kathina with a robe made beforehand is a mistake' due to a misunderstanding of the aforementioned passage. The primary point indicated by that passage is that a robe which is uncut or has fewer than five parts is unsuitable for the spreading of the Kathina. The word “Tadaheva” (on that very day) is used in that passage in relation to storage (sannidhi) and the forfeiture (nissaggiya) connection. Regarding work done on the Kathina cloth beforehand, there is no storage issue. Storage occurs only if the Community, having received the cloth, does not complete everything and spread the Kathina on that same day.

After receiving the Kathina cloth and the Community has given it to a suitable bhikkhu, the sevenfold preliminary duties must be performed. Among the preliminary duties to be performed, if any particular duty has already been done to that cloth, there is no need to do it again. Preliminary duties are to be performed only if they have not been done. If a robe that has been sewn and dyed is received, the only preliminary duty remaining to be done is the marking with the smudge-point (kappabindu). Having done that, and having determined it and spread the Kathina with it, the spreading of the Kathina is accomplished.

In the Mahāvaggapāḷi, the method of going forth is stated as: “Evañca pana bhikkhave pabbājetabbo, upasampādetabbo, paṭhamaṃ kesamassuṃ ohāretvā kāsāyāni vatthāni acchādetvā ekaṃsaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ kārāpetvā bhikkhūnaṃ pāde vandāpetvā” and so on. If someone arrives to go forth having already shaved his hair and beard, it is not necessary to follow the aforementioned teaching literally by replanting hair on his head to cut it again, or to wait until the hair and beard grow back. Since the removal of hair and beard has already occurred, he should be made to don the robes and be given the going forth.

Similarly, if a Kathina cloth is received where the preliminary duties have already occurred, there is no need to perform those preliminary duties again. If there is an unfinished preliminary duty, it should be completed and the Kathina spread. Just as the going forth of one who has already shaved his hair and beard is valid, the spreading of the Kathina done with a robe where the preliminary duties were finished beforehand is also valid. There is no obstacle caused by it being done beforehand. Therefore, in the Vinaya Commentary:

“Evaṃ dinne pana kaṭhine sace taṃ kaṭhina dussaṃ niṭṭhitaparikammameva hoti. Iccetaṃ kusalaṃ. No ce niṭṭhitaparikammaṃ ahaṃ theroti vā bahussutoti vā ekenā pi akātuṃ na labhati. Sabbeheva sannipatitvā dhovanasibbanarajanāni niṭṭhāpetabbāni”

The meaning is: "When the Kathina is thus given, if that Kathina cloth is already finished in its preparation, that is well. If it is not finished in its preparation, even a single bhikkhu is not allowed to refrain, thinking 'I am an Elder' or 'I am learned.' All must assemble and complete the washing, sewing, and dyeing."

That passage is also mentioned in the same way in the Pāḷi muttaka vinaya vinicchaya of the Venerable Sariputta, the bearer of the Tipitaka. That passage is explained in the Vinayālaṃkāra ṭīkāva as follows:

“Sace taṃ kaṭhinadussaṃ niṭṭhitaparikammameva hotīti iminā kaṭhinadussaṃ nāma na kevalaṃ pakati sāṭakameva: atha kho pariniṭṭhitasattavidhapubbaniccaṃ cīvarampi hotīti daseti (tasmā niṭṭhita cīvarampi hotīti dasseti.) tasmā niṭṭhitacīvarasmiṃ dinne sattavidhapubbakiccakaraṇena attho natthi. Kevalaṃ paccuddharaṇādīni yeva kātabbāni. Sace pana kiñci apariniṭṭhitā hoti. Antamaso kappa bindumattampi. Taṃ niṭṭhāpetvā paccuddharaṇādīni kātabbāni.”

The meaning is as follows: "By the passage “Sace taṃ kaṭhinadussaṃ niṭṭhita-parikammameva hoti”, it is shown that what is called Kathina cloth is not merely raw cloth; it can also be a robe with the sevenfold preliminary duties completed. Therefore, when a finished robe is given (for the Kathina), there is no need for the sevenfold preliminary duties. Only the relinquishing and so forth should be done. If any preliminary duty is not finished, even if at least the mere marking with the smudge-point remains, that should be completed and then the relinquishing should be done."

That the Kathina should be spread with a robe that the donor has previously cut, sewn, and dyed is shown in the Vajirabuddhi ṭīkāva as follows:

Pariniṭṭhitapubbakaraṇameva ce dāyako saṅghassa deti. Sampaṭicchitvā kammavācāya dātabbaṃ. Tena ca tasmiṃ yeva sīmāmaṇḍale adhiṭṭhahitvā attharitvā saṅgho anumodāpetabbo. Katapubbakaraṇassa puna kattabbābhāvato.

The meaning is: If the donor gives to the Community a cloth that has its preliminary work already completed, the Community should accept it and give it to the Kathina-spreader by a formal act. The Kathina-spreader (if the boundary-circle is also part of the boundary where the rains were spent) should determine it and spread the Kathina in that very boundary-circle and have the Community express appreciation. This should be done because there is nothing further to be done to a cloth that has its preliminary duties already completed.

Furthermore, in the Vajirabuddhi ṭīkāva, regarding the passage “Tadaheva sañchinnena samaṇḍalīkatena” from the Mahāvagga pāḷiya, it is stated: “Tadaheva sañchinnenāti saṅghena kaṭhinatthārakassa kammavācaṃ vatvā dinneneva tadaheva sañchinnena samaṇḍalīkatena bhavitabbaṃ -pe- na dāyakena dīyamānaṃ”. This indicates that the requirement applies to the Kathina cloth given by the Community to the Kathina-spreader after reciting the formal act, and not to the cloth while it is in the hands of the donor. No matter how long the donor keeps it and performs the sewing etc., it is the donor's storage and not the storage of the Community of monks; therefore, a robe sewn beforehand is not something that should not be taken for the spreading of the Kathina. Regarding the Kathina, it is stated in the “Mātikaṭṭha kathāvehi” as follows:

Sace purimavassaṃ vutthānaṃ gahaṭṭhapabbajitesu yo koci dhammena samena cīvaraṃ deti, iminā kaṭhinaṃ attharathāti, taṃ khandhake vuttāya ñattidutiyakammavācāya kaṭhinatthārārahassa bhikkhuno dātabbaṃ. Tena tadaheva pañca vā atirekāni vā khaṇḍāni chinditvā saṅghāṭi vā uttarāsaṅgo vā antaravāsako vā kātabbo. Sesabhikkhūhi tassa sahāyehi bhavitabbaṃ. Sace katacīvarameva uppajjati sundarameva. Acchinnā sibbitaṃ pana na vaṭṭati.

The meaning is: If anyone among householders or those gone forth gives a robe righteously and fairly to the monks who have completed the first rains-residence, saying 'Spread the Kathina with this,' it should be given to a bhikkhu suitable for the spreading of the Kathina by the Formal Statement followed by one proclamation as stated in the Khandhaka. By that bhikkhu, on that very day, it should be cut into five or more parts and made into an outer robe, an upper robe, or an inner robe. The remaining monks should be assistants to that bhikkhu. If a robe already made is produced, it is excellent. However, a robe that is uncut and unsewn is not suitable.

Thus, the fact that a robe previously cut, sewn, and dyed by donors is suitable for the Kathina is shown in various places in the Commentaries and Sub-commentaries. This is not due to an inability to understand or a misunderstanding of the Buddha's word “Na aññat‍ra pañcakena vā atireka pañcakena vā tadaheva sañchinnena samaṇḍalīkatena atthataṃ hoti kaṭhinaṃ”, but rather from understanding its meaning exactly according to the Buddha's intention.

These Commentaries and Sub-commentaries were written not in a time like the present where monks do not show much respect for the Discipline or use Vinaya texts, but in a time when monks followed the Vinaya training rules to the letter with great care and respect, and performed the learning and teaching of the Discipline with devotion. There is no doubt that during that time, not just the raw cloth but also robes previously finished and offered by donors were taken for the spreading of the Kathina. Therefore, it should be known that spreading the Kathina with robes made beforehand is not a new thing that started today or yesterday.

Some say, "There is nothing in the Buddha's teaching about taking robes sewn beforehand for the Kathina; it is a commentarial opinion." Regarding the Discipline, there is no such thing as a 'commentarial opinion' versus a 'Buddha's opinion.' The commentators do not have the power to add to, subtract from, or change the Discipline. Those Venerable Teachers do not say anything contrary to the Buddha's opinion. What is done by the Commentary is the clarification of the Buddha's opinion regarding various matters. Therefore, it should be known that both the taking of a robe sewn beforehand and the taking of cloth for the Kathina are indeed the Buddha's opinion.
