The Act of Banishment

The "Act of Banishment" (Pabbājanīya Kamma) refers to the removal of a bhikkhu from the monastery and village where he resides. It is to be carried out against bhikkhus who are corrupters of families. This formal act was first performed against the bhikkhus Assaji and Punabbasu who resided at Kīṭāgiri. Assaji and Punabbasu were two members of the group of six (chabbaggiya) bhikkhus. When performing this act, bring the bhikkhu who corrupts families into the midst of the Community, accuse him, remind him of the offense, and make him acknowledge the transgression before proceeding.

The Formal Motion for the Act of Banishment

suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ime (assaji punabbasukā) bhikkhu kuladūsakā pāpasamācārā. Imesaṃ pāpakā samācārā dissanti ceva suyyanti ca. Kulāni ca imehi duṭṭhāni dissanti ceva suyyanti ca. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ. Saṅgho (assaji punabbasukānaṃ) bhikkhūnaṃ (kīṭāgirismā) pabbājanīya kammaṃ kareyya. Na (assaji punabbasukehi) (kīṭāgirismiṃ) vatthabbanti. Esā ñatti.

There are eighteen duties to be observed by a bhikkhu against whom the Act of Banishment has been performed. When he has obediently fulfilled these eighteen duties and conducted himself properly, and requests the Community to revoke the Act of Banishment, the Community shall revoke it. Assaji and Punabbasu, however, did not conduct themselves properly under the Act of Banishment and began to revile the bhikkhus who had performed the act.

If a bhikkhu, having had the Act of Banishment performed against him for corrupting families, does not leave the monastery and reviles the bhikkhus who performed the act, saying, "These bhikkhus follow a wrong course through favoritism, etc. For such an offense they perform the Act of Banishment against some, but not against others," then other bhikkhus who see or hear this must admonish that bhikkhu up to three times to leave the monastery and to cease reviling the Community. If he does not desist, he must be brought before the Community and admonished. If he still does not desist before the Community, the Act of Formal Admonition (Samanubhāsana) must be performed against him.

Upon the conclusion of this formal motion, the bhikkhus who revile those who performed the Act of Banishment incur an offense of Saṅghādisesa.

Revocation of the Act of Banishment

If a bhikkhu against whom the Act of Banishment was performed resided in a certain monastery and corrupted families in a certain village, he must not wander for alms in that village while residing in that monastery. While residing in that monastery, he must not wander for alms in any other village. Nor should he wander for alms in that village while residing in another monastery. The bhikkhu under the Act of Banishment must fulfill the prescribed eighteen duties. There is no fixed time for fulfilling these duties; even five days may suffice. Having fulfilled the duties, that bhikkhu should approach the Community, arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, pay homage at the feet of the senior bhikkhus, sit in the squatting position, and with joined palms, make the request for the revocation of the Act of Banishment.

The Formula for Requesting Revocation

Ahaṃ bhante! saṅghena pabbājanīya kammakato. Sammā vattāmi. Lomaṃ pātemi. Netthāraṃ vattāmi. Pabbājanīya kammassa paṭippassaddhiṃ yācāmi. Dutiyampi -pe- yācāmi. Tatiyampi -pe- yācāmi.

After the revocation of the Act of Banishment, that bhikkhu is permitted to reside in that monastery. However, that bhikkhu must not accept the four requisites from the families he corrupted. Even if that bhikkhu later becomes an Arahant, he must not accept requisites from those families even after his attainment. For as long as he lives, requisites from those families are unallowable for him. If the people of those families ask, "Venerable sir, why do you not accept the requisites we offer?" and he replies, "Because I previously corrupted your families," and those people say, "We do not give because of that, but now we give out of respect for your virtue," then there is no offense in accepting requisites from them thereafter.
