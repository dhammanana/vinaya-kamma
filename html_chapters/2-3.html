Taking a Preceptor

After undertaking the novice virtue, the one who has received the going-forth should pay homage to the Preceptor, sit in the squatting position, and take the Preceptor by reciting three times the phrase: “Upajjhāyo me bhante hohi”. When the pupil has spoken thus, the Preceptor should accept it by saying: “Pāsādikena sampādehi”.

According to the word of the Buddha: “Upajjhāyo bhikkhave! saddhivihārikamhi putta cittaṃ upaṭṭhapessati. Saddhivihāriko upajjhāyamhi pitūcittaṃ upaṭṭhapessati. Evaṃ te aññamaññaṃ sagāravā sappatissā sabhāgavuttikā viharantā imasmiṃ dhammavinaye vuddhiṃ virūḷhiṃ vepullaṃ āpajjanti”, the novice should regard the Preceptor as his father, and with great respect and reverence for the Preceptor, live in complete obedience to him. The Preceptor should also regard his co-resident novice as a son and be compassionate toward him. The Preceptor should train the new novice in wearing the robes and carrying the bowl, etc., by having him reside in his presence. Until these are mastered, he should protect the new novice like one protects an infant, without sending him on invitations and such. He should teach the training rules that a novice must observe. The novice also—

“Anujānāmi bhikkhave! dasabhaṅgehi samannāgataṃ sāmaṇeraṃ nāsetuṃ, pāṇātipāti hoti. Adinnādāyi hoti, ab‍rahmacārī hoti. Musāvādi hoti, majjapāyi hoti, buddhassa avaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, dhammassa avaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, saṅghassa avaṇṇaṃ bhāsati, micchādiṭṭhiko hoti, bhikkhunī dusako hoti”

—should live obediently without breaking even one of these ten factors for expulsion mentioned above. If even one of these is violated, the entire novice virtue, beginning from the going for refuge, is broken. The taking of the Preceptor is also nullified. He loses his right to the lodging he received in the monastery and to the gains of the Community. If he does not establish himself in future restraint and take the refuges again, that novice should be expelled by having his robes removed and being driven out.

If a novice who has committed a defeat acknowledges his fault and wishes to be established in restraint again, he should be made to take the refuges again and take a Preceptor again. The going for refuge of novices is like the formal act of Higher Ordination (upasampadā-kammavācā) for bhikkhus. Just as the undertaking of the fourfold purification of virtue occurs for a bhikkhu through the recitation of the ordination kammavācā, the undertaking of the ten precepts for novices occurs through the going for refuge itself. However, for the sake of establishing future restraint and for the sake of firming the commitment, after the going for refuge, he should also be made to undertake the ten training rules. If a novice commits a fault that does not belong to the ten factors for expulsion, some form of punishment such as the water-punishment or sand-punishment should be imposed. By undergoing the punishment and being established in future restraint, his virtue is purified. There is no fault in not undertaking the training rules again.
