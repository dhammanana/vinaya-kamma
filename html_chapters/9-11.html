Two Obstacles

There are two obstacles to be known concerning the kaṭhina: the obstacle of dwelling (āvāsa-paḷibodha) and the obstacle of robes (cīvara-paḷibodha). The obstacle of dwelling is the necessity to reside in the monastery where the kaṭhina was spread, and if one departs from it, to maintain the expectation of returning. The obstacle of robes is that the bhikkhu's robe is not yet made or not yet finished, or that there is an expectation of obtaining a robe.

“Katame ca bhikkhave! dve kaṭhinassa paḷibodhā? Āvāsapaḷibodho ca cīvarapaḷibodho ca kathañca bhikkhave! āvāsapaḷibodho hoti? Idha bhikkhave! bhikkhu vasati vā yasmiṃ āvāse sāpekkhā vā pakkamati paccessati evaṃ kho bhikkhave! āvāsapaḷibodho hoti. Kathaṃ ca bhikkhave! cīvarapaḷibodho hoti? Idha bhikkhave! bhikkhuno cīvaraṃ akataṃ vā hoti vippakataṃ vā cīvarāsā vā anupacchinnā evaṃ kho bhikkhave! cīvarapaḷibodho hoti. Ime kho bhikkhave! kaṭhinassa dve paḷibodhā.”

Thus, the two obstacles are declared in the Kaṭhinakkhandhaka. The obstacle of dwelling (āvāsa-paḷibodha) persists as long as there is an expectation of residing in the monastery where the kaṭhina was spread. The obstacle of dwelling ceases when the expectation regarding the monastery is abandoned. The obstacle of robes (cīvara-paḷibodha) persists as long as there is a desire to make or obtain a robe. The obstacle of robes ceases when the robe is made, or when the cloth is destroyed, or when the expected robe is not received and the expectation is broken. The aforementioned eight types of kaṭhinoddhāra (removal of the kaṭhina) occur when these two obstacles are cut off. For a bhikkhu who has cut off the obstacle of dwelling and departed from the monastery, the validity of the kaṭhina-spreading remains until the obstacle of robes is cut off. After the obstacle of robes is cut off, that validity ends. Thereafter, that bhikkhu does not have the five advantages (pañcānisaṃsa). For a bhikkhu whose obstacle of robes has been cut off, the kaṭhinoddhāra occurs simultaneously with the abandonment of the expectation regarding the monastery.
