The Period of Kathina Spreading and the Period of Benefits

“Kaṭhinassa atthāramaso jānitabboti. Vassānassa pacchimo māso jānitabboti.” This being a Pali passage from the Parivara, the last month of the rainy season should be known as the period for spreading the Kathina. That is: the twenty-nine days from the new moon of Vap to the full moon of Il. The efficacy of the Monastic Procedure of spreading the Kathina lasts from the day the Kathina is spread until the full moon Poya day of Medin, which is the last day of the winter season. Until then, the five benefits declared for bhikkhus who have spread the Kathina are present.

If there are extra robes that have not been determined or relinquished, their determination or relinquishment should be performed on that day. For all bhikkhus, whether they have spread the Kathina or not, the twenty-nine days of the last month of the rainy season constitute the robe period (cīvara-kāla). For bhikkhus who have spread the Kathina, the entire five months from the new moon of Vap until the full moon day of Medin constitute the robe period. During the robe period, keeping extra robes does not incur an offense.
