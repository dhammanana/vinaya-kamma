Robe Designation by Proxy

Take the robe to be designated by proxy, sit squatting near a bhikkhu, and saying “imaṃ cīvaraṃ tuyhaṃ vikappemi” three times, give the robe to the other bhikkhu. Doing so is designation by proxy. The bhikkhu who has received it should say “mayhaṃ santakaṃ paribhuñja vā vissajjehi vā yathāpaccayaṃ karohi” three times and return it. That is the withdrawal (paccuddhāra). When there is more than one robe, you should say “imāni cīvarāni tuyhaṃ vikappemi”. During the withdrawal, you should say “mayhaṃ santakāni paribhuñja vā vissajjehi vā yathāpaccayaṃ karohi”. When designating by proxy robes that are outside one's reach, you should state their location and say “etaṃ cīvaraṃ tuyhaṃ vikappemi”, and if there are many robes, you should say “etāni cīvarāni tuyhaṃ vikappemi”. The withdrawal is as before.

There is no offense if pillowcases, bedsheets, etc., are not designated. When designating a robe as a supplementary cloth (parikkhāra-coḷa), if it is one, you should designate it by saying “imaṃ cīvaraṃ parikkhāracolaṃ adhiṭṭhāmi”, and if there are many, by saying “imāni cīvarāni parikkhāracolāni adhiṭṭhāmi”. When designating robes that are outside one's reach, you should designate them by saying “etaṃ cīvaraṃ parikkhāra-colaṃ adhiṭṭhāmi”, and “etāni cīvarāni parikkhāracolāni adhiṭṭhāmi”.
