Combined Probation (Agghasamodhāna parivāsa).

Among the thirteen (Saṅghādisesa) offenses, the first and second are easily committed. One may even fall into them without realization. Consequently, the elders of old would fulfill the duties and penance once every year or every few years and undergo rehabilitation (Abbhāna) to ensure their purity. For a worldling bhikkhu, it is no simple task to remain at all times free from offense. Thus, a bhikkhu may incur many offenses; this is especially likely for those who are not well-versed in the Discipline.

A bhikkhu may have many offenses that were not concealed (Appaṭicchanna). He may also have many concealed (Paṭicchanna) offenses of one day's duration. Similarly, he may have many concealed offenses for durations of two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten, eleven, twelve, thirteen, or fourteen days. For those bhikkhus who arise before dawn, pay homage to the Triple Gem, and confess their offenses daily, there can be no concealed offenses. For those bhikkhus who pay homage and confess daily in the evening, concealed offenses cannot exceed one day's duration.

For bhikkhus who perform the (Uposatha) without fail, concealed offenses cannot exceed fourteen days. For a bhikkhu who does none of these, it is not easy to measure the period for which his offenses have been concealed. A bhikkhu who has both concealed and unconcealed offenses must first undergo probation (Parivāsa) for the concealed offenses to be purified. Thereafter, combining both types of offenses, he must undergo the six-day penance (Mānatta) for all of them. A bhikkhu who has many offenses—some concealed for one day, some for two days, and so on—should combine the offenses concealed for shorter periods with those concealed for the longest period, and undergo probation for the duration of that longest period. By doing so, purity is attained from all of them. The probation undertaken by combining offenses concealed for shorter durations with those concealed for the longest duration is called ‘Combined Probation’ (Agghasamodhāna parivāsa).

The method of its fulfillment is as follows: Herein is shown the procedure for undergoing probation by combining other offenses with those concealed for ten days. Following this, you should understand how to undergo probation for other durations of concealment, whether greater or lesser.

Formula for Announcing the Probation.

Announce this three times. Thereafter, suspend (Nikkhepa) the probation, go to a suitable place, and fulfill it by undertaking it daily until the days are complete. The formula for suspension is: “Parivāsaṃ nikkhipāmi. Vattaṃ nikkhipāmi” (I suspend the probation. I suspend the duty). Recite this three times. The suspension of the duties of probation or penance may be performed in the presence of a single bhikkhu. If you forget to suspend the duty or penance before the Community within the boundary, you must go to a single bhikkhu and suspend it. If that bhikkhu is one to whom you have not previously announced your probation, you must announce it first and then suspend it. If he is a bhikkhu who was present when you announced it within the boundary, you may suspend it without announcement.

When announcing the duty or penance to a single bhikkhu, say “Vedayatīti maṃ āyasmā dhāretu”; when announcing to two bhikkhus, say “Vedayāmahaṃ āyasmantā dhārentu”; and when announcing to three bhikkhus, say “Vedayatīti maṃ āyasmanto dhārentu”.

When requesting the duties or penance, when reciting the formal act statement, and when making the announcement, use “Ekādasāhapaṭicchannāyo dvādasāhapaṭicchannāyo terasāha paṭicchannāyo cuddasāhapaṭicchannāyo” for offenses concealed from eleven to fourteen days. For offenses concealed for fifteen days, use “Pakkhapaṭicchannāyo”. For concealment from sixteen to twenty-nine days, use “Atirekapakkhapaṭicchannāyo”. For offenses concealed for one month, use “Māsapaṭicchannāyo”. Beyond that, use “Atirekamāsapaṭicchannāyo dvemāsapaṭicchannāyo atireka dvemāsapaṭicchannāyo” and so forth. For offenses concealed for one year, use “Ekasaṃvacchara paṭicchannāyo”. Beyond that, use “Atireka ekasaṃvacchara dvisaṃvacchara atireka dvisaṃvacchara” and so forth.

Upon completion of the probation, the penance (Mānatta) must be undertaken for the offenses for which probation was served as well as for those that were unconcealed. If the probation had been suspended, you must re-undertake it within the boundary and announce it to the Community before receiving the penance. Only one who is currently maintaining the undertaking of probation is eligible to receive the penance. If the penance is received while the probation remains suspended, it is not validly received. Therefore, the penance fulfilled in such a manner is fruitless. Furthermore, the days of probation may sometimes become invalid due to a 'break in the nights' (Ratticcheda). If even one day is missing from the required duration of probation, that person is not eligible to receive the penance. If the penance is taken and fulfilled without completing the probation, it is fruitless. Therefore, probation must be served with great care. If there is doubt regarding any day due to a break in the nights, the duty must be served again for those days, and the penance should only be taken once certain of completion.
