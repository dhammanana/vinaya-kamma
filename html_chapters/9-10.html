The Eight Topics

Although the spreading of the Kathina is performed by one bhikkhu, it also pertains to the bhikkhus who rejoice in it. Therefore, in the Parivāra Pāli, it was declared: “Dvinnaṃ puggalānaṃ atthataṃ hoti kaṭhinaṃ, atthārakassa ca anumodakassa ca”, meaning ‘The Kathina is spread for two persons: the spreader and the rejoicer.’

There are eight conditions by which that Kathina-spreading procedure, which pertains to both the spreader and the rejoicer, ceases, loses its validity, or becomes annulled. The annulment of the Kathina-spreading is called ‘Kaṭhinuddhāra’ or ‘Kaṭhinubbhāra’. Upon the occurrence of Kaṭhinuddhāra, the five benefits (pañcānisamsa) obtained from the Kathina-spreading are also lost.

“Aṭṭhimā bhikkhave mātikā kaṭhinassa ubbhārāya, pakkamanantikā, niṭṭhānantikā, sanniṭṭhānantikā, nāsanantikā, savanantikā, āsāvacchedikā, sīmātikkantikā, sahubbhārāti”

Thus, in the Kathinakkhandhaka of the Mahāvagga Pāli, eight topics concerning the annulment of the Kathina (Kaṭhinuddhāra) have been declared. What is indicated by ‘mātikaṃ jānāti’ among the qualifications of a person suitable for Kathina-spreading is the knowledge of these eight topics. The topics should be known as follows:

1. Pakkamanantikā (Ending with Departure).

If a bhikkhu, having completed his robe, takes it and departs from the place where he observed the rains-residence with no intention of returning, then the annulment of the Kathina (Kaṭhinuddhāra) occurs for that bhikkhu as soon as he crosses the boundary of the precincts. Thereafter, that bhikkhu loses the five benefits (pañcānisamsa) obtained from the spreading of the Kathina. This annulment of the Kathina is called ‘Pakkamanantika’ because it ends with departing from the monastery.

2. Niṭṭhānantikā (Ending with Completion).

If a bhikkhu, having received cloth at the place where he observed the rains-residence, takes it and goes to another monastery, and outside the boundary of the rains-residence, he resolves, “I will not return to the monastery where I observed the rains-residence,” then the validity of the Kathina-spreading (Kaṭhinatthāra) for that bhikkhu lasts only until he completes making a robe from the cloth he took. As soon as the work on the robe is completed, the annulment of the Kathina (Kaṭhinuddhāra) occurs for that bhikkhu. This annulment of the Kathina is called ‘Niṭṭhānantika’ because it ends with the completion of the robe.

3. Sanniṭṭhānantikā (Ending with Resolution).

If a bhikkhu, having taken the cloth he received from the place where he observed the rains-residence and departed from the monastery, resolves, “I will not return to the place where I observed the rains-residence, nor will I make this robe,” then the annulment of the Kathina (Kaṭhinuddhāra) occurs for that bhikkhu. This annulment of the Kathina is called ‘Sanniṭṭhānantika’ because it ends with the resolution.

4. Nāsanantikā (Ending with Loss).

If a bhikkhu, having received robe-cloth at the place where he observed the rains-residence and departed, resolves there, “I will make this robe right here, and I will not return to the place where I observed the rains-residence,” and while he is making that robe, the robe is lost by theft or in any other way, then the annulment of the Kathina (Kaṭhinuddhāra) occurs for that bhikkhu. This annulment of the Kathina is called ‘Nāsanantikā’ because it ends with the loss.

5. Savanantikā (Ending with Hearing).

A bhikkhu for whom the Kathina has been spread takes the cloth he received and departs with the intention of returning to the monastery later. There, the bhikkhu thinks, ‘I will make this robe right here.’ The bhikkhu makes the robe there. While that bhikkhu is staying, having completed the work on the robe, the news reaches him that ‘the Kathina has been annulled by the Community of monks in the monastery.’ Thereupon, the annulment of the Kathina (Kaṭhinuddhāra) also occurs for that bhikkhu. This annulment of the Kathina is called ‘Savanantikā’ because it ends with hearing the news.

6. Āsāvacchedikā (Ending with the Cutting Off of Hope).

A bhikkhu for whom the Kathina has been spread, not having received robes at the place where he observed the rains-residence, departs with the hope of receiving robes. Outside the monastery where he observed the rains-residence, that bhikkhu thinks, “I will not return to the place where I observed the rains-residence; I will stay here and hope for robes.” That bhikkhu stays there, reaching the place where he hopes for robes. If robes are not received from the place of hope, and that bhikkhu’s hope is cut off, then the annulment of the Kathina (Kaṭhinuddhāra) occurs for that bhikkhu. This annulment of the Kathina is called ‘Āsāvacchedikā’ because it occurs by the cutting off of hope.

7. Sīmātikkantikā (Ending with Transgression of the Boundary).

A bhikkhu for whom the Kathina has been spread takes the robe-cloth and departs with the intention of returning to the monastery. He makes the robe there. With the intention that ‘after the work on the robe is finished, that bhikkhu will return to the monastery,’ he stays there until the end of the Kathina annulment period. The annulment of the Kathina (Kaṭhinuddhāra) occurs for that bhikkhu by the expiration of the time. This annulment of the Kathina is called ‘Sīmātikkantikā’ because it occurs by transgressing the boundary, which is the last day of the robe period.

8. Sahubbhārā (Simultaneous Annulment).

A bhikkhu for whom the Kathina has been spread takes the robe and departs with the intention of returning to that monastery. He completes the robe and spends time there with the hope of returning, and then returns to the monastery at the time when the bhikkhus in the monastery annul the Kathina. The annulment of the Kathina (Kaṭhinuddhāra) occurs for that bhikkhu simultaneously with the annulment for the bhikkhus in the monastery. This annulment of the Kathina is called ‘Sahubbhārā’ because it occurs simultaneously with the annulment for the bhikkhus in the monastery. Details regarding the annulments of the Kathina should be known from the Kaṭhinakkhandhaka.
