The Formal Act of Admonition

If a certain bhikkhu attempts to cause a schism in the Community, or persists in taking up a matter that leads to a schism, the bhikkhus who meet that bhikkhu attempting the schism should advise him: "Venerable, do not attempt to cause a schism in the harmonious Community." You should advise him thus a second and a third time. Bhikkhus who hear reports of such a bhikkhu should also seek out the bhikkhu attempting the schism and advise him. If he hears the advice but does not relinquish his position, he commits an offence of wrongdoing (dukkaṭa). Bhikkhus who hear of the bhikkhu attempting the schism but do not advise him also commit an offence of wrongdoing (dukkaṭa). If the bhikkhu attempting the schism does not relinquish his position even after being advised, the other bhikkhus should bring him, even by force, into the midst of the Community and perform the Formal Act of Admonition (samanubhāsana kamma).

In performing this Formal Act of Admonition, if the bhikkhu attempting the schism does not relinquish his view, an offence of wrongdoing (dukkaṭa) is incurred at the conclusion of the Motion (ñatti). At the conclusion of the first proclamation (anusāvana), a grave offence (thullaccaya) is incurred. At the conclusion of the second proclamation, a grave offence (thullaccaya) is also incurred. At the conclusion of the transaction statement, an offence entailing initial and subsequent meetings of the Community (saṅghādisesa) is incurred.

The Formal Act of Admonition for Bhikkhus who side with Schismatics.

Bhikkhus who side with a bhikkhu attempting a schism should also be advised by other bhikkhus not to do so. You should advise them a second and a third time; if they do not accept the advice, they commit an offence of wrongdoing (dukkaṭa). Bhikkhus who know of this but do not advise them also commit an offence of wrongdoing (dukkaṭa). If they do not accept the advice, you should bring those bhikkhus into the midst of the Community, even by force, and advise them. If they still do not accept, the Formal Act of Admonition should be performed.

The Formal Act of Admonition for Difficult-to-admonish Bhikkhus.

When a bhikkhu is being advised by other bhikkhus according to the training rules laid down by the Buddha, such as: "Venerable, this should be done, this should not be done, you have fallen into such-and-such an offence," if that bhikkhu does not allow himself to be spoken to, saying: "Venerables, do not say anything to me, whether good or bad; you should mind your own business. I will not say anything to you, whether good or bad; do not say anything to me," then the bhikkhus should advise that bhikkhu not to act in that manner. You should advise him thus a second and a third time. If he does not accept the advice, he commits an offence of wrongdoing (dukkaṭa).

If the bhikkhus know of this but do not advise him, they also commit an offence of wrongdoing (dukkaṭa). If that bhikkhu does not accept the advice, he should be brought into the midst of the Community and advised. You should advise him a second and a third time. If he does not accept, the Formal Act of Admonition should be performed.
