The Act of Charging with Delusion

Certain bhikkhus, when the Patimokkha is being recited, do not listen attentively and subsequently commit an offense, saying: "We did not know earlier that this was a fault—that there is an offense for this; we have only now learned that such a training rule exists." They thus attempt to convince the Community that the offense was committed through ignorance. The Act of Charging with Delusion is the formal act prescribed for the purpose of disciplining such bhikkhus.

Ignorance of a training rule is not a valid ground for exemption from an offense. Therefore, have the bhikkhu who claims to have committed the offense through ignorance confess that offense. If the bhikkhus are aware that the offending bhikkhu has attended the recitation of the Patimokkha at least two or three times previously, the Community should carry out the Act of Charging with Delusion against him.

The formal act-statement for performing it is as follows:

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho. Ayaṃ (tisso) bhikkhu pātimokkhe uddissamāne na sādhukaṃ aṭṭikat‍vā manasikaroti. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ. Saṅgho (tissassa) bhikkhuno mohaṃ āropey‍ya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (tisso) bhikkhu pātimokkhe uddissamāne na sādhukaṃ aṭṭikat‍vā manasikaroti. Saṅgho (tissassa) bhikkhuno mohaṃ āropeti. Yassāyasmato khamati. (tissassa) bhikkhuno mohassa āropanā. So tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati so bhāsey‍ya.

Āropito saṅghena (tissassa) bhikkhuno moho. Khamati saṅghassa. Tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.

If, after this formal act has been performed, the bhikkhu still claims ignorance of the training rule, he incurs a Pacittiya offense.
