The Benefits of Kathina

To bhikkhus who have spread the Kathina, there are five benefits received according to the Buddha's injunction due to that Monastic Procedure. This is called ‘Kaṭhināniṃsa’ (Benefits of Kathina). Furthermore, the robes and other requisites received due to the Kathina robe are also called Kaṭhināniṃsa. The reward received by the Kathina donor from that merit is also called by that name. It should be known that the five benefits received by the bhikkhus who have spread the Kathina, according to the Buddha's injunction, are not a result of that merit. The fivefold benefits received by the bhikkhu are declared thus in the Kaṭhinakkhandhaka:

Atthatakaṭhinānaṃ vo bhikkhave! Pañcakappissanti anāmantavāro, asamādānavāro, gaṇabhojanaṃ, yāvadatthacīvaraṃ, yo ca tattha cīvaruppādo so nesaṃ bhavissati.

Hereby it is declared that five matters become allowable for bhikkhus who have spread the Kathina due to that reason. These are as follows:-

1. Anāmantacāro (Going without prior invitation).

“Yo pana bhikkhu nimantito sa bhatto samāno santaṃ bhikkhuṃ anāpucchā purebhattaṃ vā pacchābhattaṃ vā kulesu cārittaṃ āpajjeyya aññatra samayā pācittiyaṃ tatrāyaṃ samayo cīvaradāna samayo, cīvarakārasamayo, ayaṃ tattha samayo.”

This is the sixth training rule in the Acelakavagga of the Pātimokkha. According to this training rule, the offense incurred by a bhikkhu residing in a monastery who goes to houses without asking another bhikkhu is not applicable to bhikkhus who have spread the Kathina. After the Kathina has been spread, freedom is obtained to frequent families without asking.

2. Asamādānacāro (Going without the three robes).

“Niṭṭhitacīvarasmiṃ pana bhikkhunā ubbhatasmiṃ kaṭhine. Ekarattaṃ’pi ce bhikkhu ticīvarena vippavaseyya aññatra bhikkhu sammutiyā, nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.”

This is the second training rule in the Nissaggiya Pācittiyas of the Pātimokkha. According to this training rule, the offense incurred by a bhikkhu who spends a night separated from his determined set of three robes is not applicable to bhikkhus who have spread the Kathina.

3. Gaṇabhojanaṃ (Group meal).

“Gaṇabhojane aññatra samayā pācittiyaṃ. Tatthāyaṃ samayo. Gilānasamayo cīvaradānasamayo cīvarakārasamayo addhānagamanasamayo nāvābhirūhanasamayo mahāsamayo samaṇabhattasamayo ayaṃ tattha samayo.”

This is the second training rule in the Bhojanavagga of the Pātimokkha. According to this training rule, the offense incurred by partaking in a group meal is not applicable to bhikkhus who have spread the Kathina.

4. Yāvadattha Cīvaraṃ (As many robes as one wishes).

“Niṭṭhitacīvarasmiṃ pana bhikkhunā ubbhatasmiṃ kaṭhine dasāhaparamaṃ atirekacīvaraṃ dhāretabbaṃ: taṃ atikkāmayato nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ.”

This is the first training rule in the Nissaggiya Pācittiyas. According to this training rule, the offense incurred by keeping extra robes for more than ten days is not applicable to bhikkhus who have spread the Kathina.

5. Yo ca tattha Cīvaruppādo so nesaṃ bhavissati (Whatever robe-material arises there, that will be theirs).

If, within the boundary where the Kathina has been spread, robes belonging to the Community arise as rag-robes, or are offered by devotees as belonging to the Community, or from things produced on that land, or in any other way, during the period of Kathina benefits, all of those belong to the bhikkhus who have spread the Kathina. Apart from robes received communally, other requisites do not become theirs by the power of the Kathina spreading. Those belong to everyone. The reception of these five benefits is the special advantage for bhikkhus from the spreading of the Kathina.
