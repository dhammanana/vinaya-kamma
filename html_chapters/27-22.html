Authorization of an Overseer of Monastery Attendants.

Monastery attendants are laypeople who reside in the monastery for the purpose of assisting the bhikkhus. When there is no one to assign them to various tasks, they do not perform the work. Even if they do, they do not do it properly. Therefore, the Tathāgata authorized the appointment of an Overseer of Monastery Attendants. An "Overseer of Monastery Attendants" is one who assigns the monastery attendants to their respective duties. The bhikkhu who has received the Authorization of an Overseer of Monastery Attendants must direct the monastery attendants in their various tasks.

Procedural Text.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ ārāmikapesakaṃ sammanney‍ya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, saṅgho (tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ ārāmikapesakaṃ sammannati. Yassāyasmato khamati (tissassa) bhikkhuno ārāmikapesakassa sammuti. So tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati so bhāsey‍ya.

Sammato saṅghena (tisso) bhikkhu ārāmikapesako. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.
