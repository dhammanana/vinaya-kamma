The Age of the Candidate for Higher Ordination

The age of the candidate for Higher Ordination is a matter of particular importance. “Yo pana bhikkhu jānaṃ ūnavīsati vassaṃ puggalaṃ upasampādey‍ya so ca puggalo anupasampanno. Teca bhikkhu gārayhā idaṃ tasmiṃ pācittiyaṃ” — Thus, the Tathāgata has laid down an offense of Expiation (Pācittiya) for a Preceptor who confers Higher Ordination upon a person known to be under twenty years of age. For the offense of conferring Higher Ordination on other disqualified persons, only an offense of Wrongdoing (Dukkaṭa) is prescribed, not an offense of Expiation. Therefore, we state that the age of the candidate for Higher Ordination must be considered with special care. The age of the candidate for Higher Ordination should be calculated not from birth, but from the moment of conception. This is to be understood from the following:

“Yaṃ bhikkhave! mātukucchismiṃ paṭhamaṃ cittaṃ uppannaṃ. Paṭhamaṃ viññāṇaṃ pātubhūtaṃ. Tadupādāya sāvassa jāti. Anujānāmi bhikkhave gabbhavīsaṃ upasampādetuṃ”

This is to be known from the teaching of the Monastic Procedures. A person who spent ten months in the mother's womb may be given Higher Ordination when nineteen years and two months have elapsed since birth. A person who spent nine months in the mother's womb may be given Higher Ordination after nineteen years and three months have elapsed since birth. There is no offense if one confers Higher Ordination by mistake, thinking a person who has not completed twenty years has indeed completed them. However, the Higher Ordination is not valid for the candidate whose age was misunderstood. If he, after ten years of seniority, becomes a Preceptor and confers Higher Ordination on a pupil, the Higher Ordination is valid for the pupil provided there were enough other bhikkhus present to complete the quorum for the formal act, excluding him. For that person who lives as a monk without knowing that he was ordained under twenty years of age, such living is not an obstacle to heaven or liberation. Even if he has reached old age, if he comes to know for certain that he was ordained under-age, he must receive Higher Ordination again. Since presenting oneself as one who has received Higher Ordination after knowing the truth is a deception, it becomes an obstacle for him.

“Anāpatti ūnavīsativassaṃ paripuṇṇavīsati saññīti ettha kiñcāpi upasampādentassa anāpatti puggalo pana anupasampannova hoti. Sace pana so dasavassaccayena aññaṃ upasampādeti. Taṃ ce muñcitvā gaṇo pūrati. Supasampanno so pi ca yāva na jānāti. Tāvassa neva saggantarāyo na mokkhantarāyo. Ñatvā pana puna upasampajjitabbaṃ” — Thus states the Vinaya Commentary.
