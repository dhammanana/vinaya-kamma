Persons to be Excluded.

A lay person, a bhikkhuni, a sikkhamana, a samanera, a samaneri, one who has rejected the training, one who has fallen into a parajika offence, one on whom the ukkhepaniya kamma (act of suspension) has been performed for not seeing an offence, one on whom the ukkhepaniya kamma has been performed for not making amends for an offence, one on whom the ukkhepaniya kamma has been performed for not giving up a wicked view, a eunuch, one who dwells in stolen communion, one who has gone over to another sect, an animal, a matricide, a parricide, a slayer of an Arahant, one who has violated a bhikkhuni, a schismatic of the Community, one who has drawn blood from a Buddha, and a hermaphrodite — these twenty-one persons are to be excluded from the boundary (atthapasa) during the Observance of Uposatha.

The term ‘atthapasa’ refers to two and a half cubits. Those persons are to be excluded from the boundary (atthapasa) of the Community of monks (Sangha) observing Uposatha. If, among these twenty-one persons, the three suspended ones (ukkhittaka) are present within the boundary (atthapasa) during the Observance of Uposatha, all bhikkhus incur a pacittiya offence. If the others are present during the Observance of Uposatha, a dukkata offence is incurred.
