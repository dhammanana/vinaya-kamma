Adhiṭṭhāna of Robes

“Niṭṭhitacīvarasmiṃ pana bhikkhunā ubbhatasmiṃ kaṭhine dasāhaparamṃ atireka cīvaraṃ dhāretabbaṃ. Taṃ atikkāmayato nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ” is a Vinaya rule; therefore, apart from special times when one is free to keep robes without making an adhiṭṭhāna or vikappana, if an extra robe is kept for more than ten days on any other day, it entails a Nissaggiya Pācittiya. An 'extra robe' refers to robes for which an adhiṭṭhāna or vikappana has not been made. There are nine kinds of robes: the saṅghāṭi, the uttarāsaṅga, the antaravāsaka, the vassikasāṭika (rain-cloth), the nisīdana (sitting-cloth), the pacchattharaṇa (covering-cloth), the kaṇḍupaṭicchādika (anti-itch cloth), the mukhapuñchana-coḷaka (mouth-wiping cloth), and the parikkhāra-coḷa (requisite-cloth). Of these, the saṅghāṭi, uttarāsaṅga, and antaravāsaka are the principal ones.

The saṅghāṭi is the double-layered robe, the uttarāsaṅga is the single-layered robe, and the antaravāsaka is the lower robe. Before making an adhiṭṭhāna for these, they must be dyed and marked with a kappabindu. The kappabindu should be placed on all four corners of the robe, or on three, or on two, or on one corner, in blue color, about the size of a peacock's eye. For the saṅghāṭi, one should resolve, “Imaṃ saṅghāṭiṃ adhiṭṭhāmi”; for the uttarāsaṅga, “Imaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ adhiṭṭhāmi”; and for the antaravāsaka, “Imaṃ antaravāsakaṃ adhiṭṭhāmi”. If one makes the adhiṭṭhāna while they are outside one's reach, one should consider their location and resolve: Etaṃ saṅghāṭiṃ adhiṭṭhāmi, etaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ adhiṭṭhāmi, etaṃ antaravāsakaṃ adhiṭṭhāmi.

Sizes of Robes

Robes that are either smaller or larger than the prescribed size cannot be designated as saṅghāṭi, uttarāsaṅga, or antaravāsaka. According to the final section, the saṅghāṭi and uttarāsaṅga, measured by the hand of the bhikkhu making the adhiṭṭhāna, should be four cubits and one short cubit in length, and two cubits and one short cubit in width. The antaravāsaka should be four cubits and one short cubit in length, and two cubits in width. If they are smaller than these sizes, they cannot be used for the adhiṭṭhāna of the three robes (ticīvarādhiṭṭhāna). There is no fault if they are larger. However, every robe must be smaller than the Sugata-cīvara (the robe of the Fortunate One).

Breaking the Adhiṭṭhāna of Robes

The ticīvarādhiṭṭhāna is also broken by the nine reasons stated for breaking the adhiṭṭhāna of the bowl. The adhiṭṭhāna is broken by a hole occurring in the saṅghāṭi and uttarāsaṅga, within a span from both ends of the length, and within eight inches from both ends of the width, if the hole is about the size of a little finger's nail. For the antaravāsaka, it is broken by a hole of the stated size occurring within a span from both ends of the length, and within four inches from both ends of the width. If not even a single thread remains in the middle of the hole, the adhiṭṭhāna is not broken. A robe whose adhiṭṭhāna has thus been broken must be mended and repaired, and then resolved again within ten days.
