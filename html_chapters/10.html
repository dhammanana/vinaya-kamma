Unconcealed Mānatta

A bhikkhu who has fallen into a grave offense must inform another bhikkhu of that fact on the very same day. If he falls into an offense during the night, he must inform another bhikkhu that same night before the rise of dawn on the following day. If he fails to inform on that day without any obstacle, there is an additional offense of wrongdoing. The informing bhikkhu should approach another bhikkhu, arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, squat down, raise his joined palms, and inform him saying: “Tuyhaṃ santike ekaṃ āpattiṃ āvikaromi” or “ācikkhāmi” or “ārocemi” or “mama ekaṃ āpattiṃ āpannabhāvaṃ jānāhi”. Or, as is currently used during the confession of offenses, he should inform saying: “Ahaṃ bhante sabbāpattiyo āvikaromi”.

For bhikkhus who rise before dawn, pay homage to the Triple Gem, and confess their offenses daily, there is no concealment of offenses. To be rehabilitated from unconcealed grave offenses, one must observe Mānatta for six days and then undergo the Act of Rehabilitation. Regardless of the number of offenses, one may observe Mānatta for all of them at once for the sake of purification. When doing so, assemble the Community within a consecrated boundary to grant the Mānatta, which must then be observed. It should be performed as follows:

The Request for Unconcealed Mānatta

Ahaṃ bhante! Sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpattiyo āpajjiṃ. Appaṭicchannāyo. So’haṃ bhante saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ yācāmi.

Within the consecrated boundary, before the Community, arrange the upper robe over one shoulder, squat down, raise the joined palms, and request Mānatta by reciting the above phrase three times.

The Formal Act for Granting Mānatta

“Suṇātu me bhante saṅgho. Ayaṃ (Tisso) bhikkhu sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpattiyo āpajji, appaṭicchannāyo. So saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ yācati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (Tissassa) bhikkhuno tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ dadeyya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho. Ayaṃ (Tisso) bhikkhu sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpattiyo āpajji, appaṭicchannāyo. So saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ yācati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (Tissassa) bhikkhuno tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ deti. Yassāyasmato khamati, (Tissassa) bhikkhuno tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattassa dānaṃ, so tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati so bhāseyya.

Dutiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi. Suṇātu me bhante! -pe- so bhāseyya.

Tatiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi. Suṇātu me bhante! -pe- so bhāseyya.

Dinnaṃ saṅghena (Tissassa) bhikkhuno tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ. Khamati saṅghassa tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.”

Recite the formal act inserting the appropriate name.

The bhikkhu who has received Mānatta from the Community must undertake the Mānatta right there before the Community. The phrase for undertaking Mānatta is as follows:

“Mānattaṃ samādiyāmi. Vattaṃ samādiyāmi.”

Arrange the upper robe over one shoulder, squat down, pay homage to the Community, and recite the above phrase three times to complete the undertaking of Mānatta.

After the undertaking, announce the Mānatta to the Community. The phrase for announcement is as follows:

Ahaṃ bhante! Sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpattiyo āpajji’. Appaṭicchannāyo. So’haṃ bhante saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ yāciṃ. Tassa me saṅgho tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ adāsi. So’haṃ mānattaṃ carāmi. Vedayāmahaṃ bhante! Vedayatīti maṃ saṅgho dhāretu.

Announce the Mānatta to the Community three times in this manner. A bhikkhu who has thus received Mānatta from the Community within a consecrated boundary may relinquish it whenever he wishes. He may then undertake it again without receiving it from the Community a second time. While undertaking and observing Mānatta or Probation, he must announce it to every bhikkhu he meets. Failure to do so results in an interruption of nights and an offense of wrongdoing for breach of duty. Interruption of nights means the loss of that day. If a bhikkhu observing Mānatta sees another bhikkhu even at a distance and is unable to announce it to him, that day is void. Another day must be observed in place of that day. Since it is not easy to observe duties and Mānatta everywhere, relinquish the Mānatta until you begin to observe it in a suitable place. Having undertaken it and announced it to the Community within the consecrated boundary where the Mānatta was received—

“Mānattaṃ nikkhipāmi. Vattaṃ nikkhipāmi.”

Relinquish it by saying this three times. Thereafter, until he undertakes it again, that bhikkhu is a regular bhikkhu. The practices to be observed by a bhikkhu undergoing Mānatta must be performed during the period he remains under the undertaking of Mānatta.

Purification is not attained merely by observing Mānatta. After observing Mānatta for six days, the Act of Rehabilitation must be performed to attain purification. To perform an Act of Rehabilitation, a group of no fewer than twenty bhikkhus must be assembled within a consecrated boundary. Since this is not a task that can be done immediately, relinquish the Mānatta and wait after the days are completed. A bhikkhu who is currently under the undertaking of Mānatta is eligible for the Act of Rehabilitation. Therefore, when the Community has assembled and is preparing for the Act of Rehabilitation, he should again undertake the Mānatta within that consecrated boundary, announce it to the Community, and then request the Act of Rehabilitation from the Community using the following phrase:

The Request for Abbhāna

Ahaṃ bhante! Sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpattiyo āpajjiṃ appaṭicchannāyo. So’haṃ bhante saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ yāciṃ. Tassa me saṅgho tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ adāsi. So’haṃ bhante ciṇṇamānatto saṅghaṃ abbhānaṃ yācāmi.

Say this three times.

The Formal Act for Abbhāna

“Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (Tisso) bhikkhu sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpappattiyo āpajji, appaṭicchannāyo. So saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ yāci. Saṅgho (Tissassa) bhikkhuno tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ adāsi. So ciṇṇamānatto saṅghaṃ abbhānaṃ yācati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (Tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ abbheyya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (Tisso) bhikkhu sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpattiyo āpajjiṃ appaṭicchannāyo. So saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ yāci. Saṅgho (Tissassa) bhikkhuno tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ appaṭicchannānaṃ chārattaṃ mānattaṃ adāsi. So ciṇṇamānatto saṅghaṃ abbhānaṃ yācati. Saṅgho Tissaṃ bhikkhuṃ abbheti. Yassāyasmato khamati (Tissassa) bhikkhuno abbhānaṃ, so tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati so bhāseyya.

Dutiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi. Suṇātu me -pe- bhāseyya.

Tatiyampi etamatthaṃ vadāmi. Suṇātu me -pe- bhāseyya.

Abbhito saṅghena (Tisso) bhikkhu. Khamati saṅghassa tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi”

Concealment of Offenses

One who has fallen into a grave offense must inform another bhikkhu of that fact on the very same day. If he lives concealing the offense for any number of days without informing, the bhikkhu must undergo Probation for that number of days before observing Mānatta. If he performs the Act of Rehabilitation after observing only Mānatta without undergoing Probation, there is no purification regarding the concealed offenses.

“Āpatti ca hoti āpattisaññī ca. Pakatatto ca hoti pakatattasaññī ca, antarāyiko ca hoti antarāyikasaññī ca, pahu ca hoti pahusaññī ca, chādetukāmo ca hoti jādeti ca”

Concealment of offenses is shown in ten ways. If a grave offense occurs and with the perception that it is a grave offense, the bhikkhu does not inform on that day, he has concealed the offense. If he falls into an offense but does not know it and thus does not inform, he has not concealed it. If he thinks a grave offense is a light offense and does not inform, it is not concealed. If he falls into a grave offense and, not knowing whether it is grave or light, does not inform with the intention of hiding it, it is concealed.

If he is a regular bhikkhu who has not been suspended by the Community through an Act of Suspension, and with the perception that he is a regular bhikkhu, he does not inform of the offense, it is concealed. If he thinks, "I have been suspended by the Community; I am not a regular bhikkhu," and does not inform, the offense is not concealed. If he is not a regular bhikkhu but thinks he is and does not inform, the offense is not concealed.

If there is not even one of the ten obstacles that prevent going to a bhikkhu to inform of the offense, and with the perception that there is no obstacle, he does not inform, the offense is concealed. The ten obstacles are: obstacle from kings, obstacle from thieves, obstacle from fire, obstacle from water, obstacle from humans, obstacle from non-humans, obstacle from beasts, obstacle from reptiles, obstacle to life, and obstacle to the holy life. If there are obstacles on the way from where he resides to where the bhikkhu to be informed resides, and he does not go to inform, the offense is not concealed.

If he has the strength to go to a bhikkhu to inform, and knowing that, he does not go to inform, the offense is hidden. If he does not inform because he lacks the strength to go, the offense is not concealed. Even if he has the strength to go, but does not inform with the perception that he cannot go due to a minor ailment, it is not concealed.

If he does not inform with the intention of hiding the offense, he has concealed the offense. If the intention to hide arose earlier but changed later and he informs before dawn, he has not concealed it. For one who falls into an offense in a place where there are no bhikkhus and waits for the arrival of a bhikkhu to inform, even if days pass, the offense is not concealed. For one who travels to a bhikkhu of the same class to inform, even if many days pass, the offense is not concealed. If one who is waiting for a bhikkhu to inform does not inform the first bhikkhu he meets out of shame because that bhikkhu is his teacher or preceptor, he has concealed the offense. If he does not inform hostile persons who would spread the word and insult him, he has not concealed the offense.

“Āpattinukkhippamanantarāya

Pahuttatāyo tathā saññitā ca.

Jādetukāmo atha jādanāti

Jannā dasaṅgeharuṇuggamamhi.”

The meaning is: by these ten factors—the occurrence of a grave offense, being a bhikkhu who is not suspended, the absence of obstacles, the strength to go to a bhikkhu, having those four perceptions, the desire to hide the offense, and the act of hiding—the offense is concealed at the rise of dawn on the following day.
