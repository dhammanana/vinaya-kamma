The Act of Suspension for a Bhikkhu who does not wish to make amends for an offense.

While the Blessed One was staying at Ghositarama in Kosambi, the Elder Channa fell into an offense and did not wish to make amends for it. On that account, the Tathagata prescribed that the Community perform the Act of Suspension for not making amends for an offense against the Elder Channa, resulting in non-communion with the Community. This act must be performed by summoning the bhikkhu into the midst of the Community, accusing him, reminding him of the fault, and pointing out the offense.

The Formal Act (Kamma-vācā)

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (channo) bhikkhu āpattiṃ āpajjit‍vā na icchati āpattiṃ paṭikātuṃ. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (channassa) bhikkhuno āpattiyā appaṭikamme ukkhepanīya kammaṃ karey‍ya asambhogaṃ saṅghena. Esā ñatti.

Revocation of the Act of Suspension.

When the Act of Suspension for not making amends for an offense has been performed, the bhikkhu must conduct himself according to the forty-three duties, satisfy the Community, approach the Community, pay homage at the feet of the senior bhikkhus, sit in the squatting position, and with hands joined in reverence, request the revocation of the act using the following formula. When this is done, the Community shall revoke the act.

Ahaṃ bhante! saṅghena āpattiyā appaṭikamme ukkhepanīyakammakato sammā vattāmi. Lomaṃ pātemi. Netthāraṃ vattāmi. Āpattiyā appaṭikamme ukkhepanīyassa kammassa paṭippassaddhiṃ yācāmi. Dutiyampi -pe- tatiyampi -pe-
