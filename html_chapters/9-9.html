Revocation and Determination

These two matters should also be especially known by the bhikkhus who spread the Kathina. The paccuddhāra intended in this discourse on the Kathina is stated in the Parivāra as: ‘Paccuddhāro tīhi dhammehi saṅgahito. Saṅghāṭiyā uttarāsaṅgena antaravāsakena’, meaning the revocation pertaining to the three robes: the outer robe, the upper robe, and the inner robe.

At present, few bhikkhus use robes determined as the three robes (ticīvarādhiṭṭhāna). Many bhikkhus, for convenience, use robes determined as accessory cloths (parikkhāracola). A bhikkhu who uses robes in this way does not have an outer robe, an upper robe, or an inner robe to perform a revocation before determining the Kathina robe. Revoking robes determined as accessory cloths is not sufficient for this purpose. In such a situation, what should a bhikkhu do to be able to spread the Kathina? This is explained in the Vinayālaṅkāra Ṭīkā as follows:

Ticīvarādhiṭṭhānassa dupparihāriyattā sabbadā dhāretuṃ asakkonto hutvā parikkhāravolavasena dhārento’pi taṃ paccuddhāritvā āsanṇe kāle ticīvarādhiṭṭhānena adhiṭṭhahanto’ pi kaṭhinaṃ attharituṃ labhati yeva.

The meaning of this is: ‘Since it is difficult to use robes determined as the three robes at all times, even a bhikkhu who uses robes determined as accessory cloths, having revoked those robes determined as accessory cloths, and having determined them according to the method of the three robes at a time close to the spreading of the Kathina, is indeed suitable to spread the Kathina.’

Therefore, if a bhikkhu who uses robes determined as accessory cloths (parikkhāracola adhiṭṭhāna) has to receive a Kathina robe, that bhikkhu should take a double-layered robe, a single-layered robe, and an undergarment from the robes he uses as accessory cloths, and say: “Imāni cīvarāni parikkhāracolāni paccuddhārāmi” (I revoke these robes determined as accessory cloths). Having revoked them, he should then determine them one by one according to the method of the three robes (ticīvarādhiṭṭhāna vidhi). Then, he should revoke one of those three robes and, in its place, determine the Kathina robe and spread the Kathina. Only by doing so are the acts of revocation and determination (paccuddhāra-adhiṭṭhāna kamma) complete.
