Instruction given at the conclusion of the Higher Ordination

Tāvadeva jāyāmetabbā. Utuppamāṇaṃ ācikkhitabbaṃ divasabhāgo ācikkhitabbo. Saṅgīti ācikkhitabbā. Cattāro nissayā ācikkhitabbā. Cattāri akaraṇiyāni ācikkhitabbāni.

Since our Fortunate, Worthy, Perfectly Self-Enlightened Buddha, who has traversed all knowable realms and is greatly compassionate, has thus declared, and since the Great Community of monks, endowed with virtues such as good conduct, has consecrated the Venerable One with the supremely pure Higher Ordination, it has been permitted to declare these at the time of receiving the Higher Ordination in this sima (ordination hall): the measure of the shadow, the measure of the season, the division of the day, all of these, the four supports, and the four prohibitions. Therefore, please listen attentively to our words.

Venerable One, who has been consecrated with the supremely pure Higher Ordination precept! You, on the ................ day, the ................... tithi of the ................ month, in the ................ year of the Buddhist Era, corresponding to the ................ day of the ................ month, in the ................ year of the Common Era, at ................ in the ................ sima (ordination hall), were raised from the state of a novice and brought to the state of a bhikkhu with Higher Ordination by the Great Community of monks, led by ................... Bearing this in mind for the duration of your life, protect the Higher Ordination precept you have now received as you would your own life, without breaking even the smallest training rule.
