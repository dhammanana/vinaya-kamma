Observing Uposatha

There are three seasons in a year: Hemanta (winter), Gimhana (summer), and Vassana (rainy). Each season has four months. Each month has thirty days; some months have twenty-nine days. There are two fortnights in a month, called the waxing and waning halves. Each fortnight has fifteen days; some fortnights also have fourteen days. There are twelve months in a year: Il (October-November), Unduvap (November-December), Duruthu (December-January), Navam (January-February), Medin (February-March), Bak (March-April), Vesak (April-May), Poson (May-June), Esala (June-July), Nikini (July-August), Binara (August-September), and Vap (September-October). Each month has two Uposatha days. 'Uposatha' refers to the last day of a fortnight. The division of the twelve months into seasons is as follows.

“Kantikassa ca kālamhā

Yāva egguṇapuṇṇamā

Hemantakālo viññeyyo

Aṭṭha honti uposathā.”

The four months from the first day of the waning half of Il (October-November) up to the full moon of Medin (February-March) constitute the Hemanta season. There are eight Uposatha days in it.

“Egguṇassa ca kālamhā

Yāva āsaḷhapuṇṇamā

Gimhakāloti viññeyyo

Aṭṭha honti uposathā”

The period from the first day of the waning half of Medin (February-March) up to the full moon of Esala (June-July) constitutes the Gimhana season. There are eight Uposatha days in it.

“Āsaḷhassa ca kālamhā

Yāva kattikapuṇṇamā

Vassakāloti viññeyyo

Aṭṭha honti uposathā.”

The four months from the first day of the waning half of Esala (June-July) up to the full moon of Il (October-November) constitute the Vassana season. There are eight Uposatha days in it.

“Utūnaṃ pana tiṇṇaṃ

Pakkhe tatiya sattame

Cātuddasoti pātimokkhaṃ

Uddisanti nayaññuto.”

In all three seasons, the third and seventh Uposatha days are the Cātuddasī (fourteenth-day Uposatha). Out of the twenty-four Uposatha days in a year, six are Cātuddasī, and eighteen are Paṇṇarasī (fifteenth-day Uposatha).

'Observing Uposatha' refers to the proper procedure to be carried out by bhikkhus on the Uposatha day. That is: if four or more bhikkhus are assembled, the recitation of the Pātimokkha; if three or two bhikkhus are present, the declaration of purity; and if only one bhikkhu is present, the determination, "Ajjatagge uposatho" (Today is the Uposatha day).
