The Act of Suspension for Not Acknowledging an Offense.

While the Blessed One was staying at Ghositarama in Kosambi, the Elder Channa committed an offense, and when told by the bhikkhus that he had fallen into an offense, he did not wish to acknowledge it. When the bhikkhus reported this to the Tathagata, He prescribed the "Act of Suspension" for the Elder Channa. The purpose of the Act of Suspension is for the Community to exclude the offending bhikkhu from communion. When performing it, the bhikkhu who does not acknowledge the offense must be accused, reminded of the offense, and charged before the act is carried out.

The Procedural Text (Kamma-vākya)

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (channo) bhikkhu āpattiṃ āpajjit‍vā na icchati āpattiṃ passituṃ, yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (channassa) bhikkhuno āpattiyā adassane ukkhepanīyakammaṃ karey‍ya asambhogaṃ saṅghena, esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (channo) bhikkhu āpattiṃ āpajjit‍vā na icchati āpattiṃ passituṃ. Saṅgho (channassa) bhikkhuno āpattiyā adassane ukkhepanīyakammaṃ karoti asambhogaṃ saṅghena. Yassāyasmato khamati (channassa) bhikkhuno āpattiyā adassane ukkhepanīyassa kammassa karaṇaṃ. Asambhogaṃ saṅghena, so tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati, so bhāsey‍ya.

Duties to be observed by a suspended bhikkhu.

  1. He should not confer the Full Ordination as a preceptor or teacher.
  2. He should not give dependence (nissaya).
  3. He should not take a novice as a preceptor.
  4. He should not accept authorization to exhort bhikkhunis.
  5. Even if authorized, he should not exhort bhikkhunis.
  6. He should not commit that same offense for which the Act of Suspension was performed.
  7. He should not commit any other similar offense.
  8. He should not commit any worse offense.
  9. He should not disparage the formal act.
  10. He should not disparage the bhikkhus who performed the act.

11-21. He should not accept from a regular bhikkhu: salutation, rising from the seat, salutation with joined palms, proper services such as fanning, the preparation of seats, the preparation of beds, the setting out of water for washing the feet, the setting out of footstools, the setting out of foot-mats, the receiving of bowl and robes, or the scrubbing of the back during a bath.

  1. He should not accuse a regular bhikkhu of a failure in virtue.
  2. He should not accuse him of a failure in conduct.
  3. He should not accuse him of a failure in view.
  4. He should not accuse him of a failure in livelihood.
  5. He should not cause a regular bhikkhu to break with other bhikkhus.
  6. He should not wear white clothes or other lay garments.
  7. He should not wear the robes of sectarians, such as those made of kusa grass.
  8. He should associate with bhikkhus.
  9. He should train in the training of bhikkhus.
  10. He should not live in a residence under the same roof as a regular bhikkhu.
  11. He should not live in a non-residence under the same roof as a regular bhikkhu.
  12. He should not live in either a residence or a non-residence under the same roof as a regular bhikkhu.
  13. On seeing a regular bhikkhu, he should rise from his seat.
  14. He should not show disrespect to a regular bhikkhu, whether inside or outside the monastery.
  15. He should not suspend the Uposatha of a regular bhikkhu.
  16. He should not suspend the Pavarana of a regular bhikkhu.
  17. He should not issue a formal summons (savacanīya) to a regular bhikkhu.
  18. He should not take a position of leadership in the monastery.
  19. He should not ask for leave to accuse.
  20. He should not bring an accusation.
  21. He should not remind another of an offense.
  22. He should not incite bhikkhus to quarrel with other bhikkhus.

Revocation of the Act of Suspension for Not Acknowledging an Offense.

The suspended bhikkhu, having fulfilled his duties and satisfied the Community, should approach the Community, arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, pay homage at the feet of the senior bhikkhus, sit in the squatting position, and request the revocation of the act by reciting the following passage:

Ahaṃ bhante! saṅghena āpattiyā adassena ukkhepanīya kammakato sammā vattāmi. Lomaṃ pātemi. Net‍thāraṃ vattāmi. Āpattiyā adassane ukkhepanīyassa kammassa paṭippassaddhiṃ yācāmi. Dutiyampi -pe- tatiyampi -pe-

The Procedural Text (Kamma-vākya)

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (channo) bhikkhu saṅghena āpattiyā adassane ukkhepanīyakammakato sammā vattati. Lomaṃ pāteti. Net‍thāraṃ vattati. Āpattiyā adassane ukkhepanīyassa kammassa paṭippassaddhiṃ yācati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (channassa) bhikkhuno āpattiyā adassane ukkhepanīyakammaṃ paṭippassambhey‍ya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho. Ayaṃ (channo) bhikkhu saṅghena āpattiyā adassane ukkhepanīyakammakato sammā vattati. Lomaṃ pāteti. Net‍thāraṃ vattati. Āpattiyā adassane ukkhepanīya kammassa paṭippassaddhiṃ yācati. Saṅgho (channassa) bhikkhuno āpattiyā adassane ukkhepanīyakammaṃ paṭippassambheti. Yassāyasamato khamati (channassa) bhikkhuno āpattiyā adassane ukkhepanīya kammassa paṭippassaddhi, so tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati, so bhāsey‍ya.
