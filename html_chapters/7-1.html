Going with a Sattāhakaraniya.

When going forth from the place of Rains Residence with a sattāhakaraniya, one should go having resolved, while still within the boundary of the Rains Residence, "I shall go forth for this reason requiring a sattāhakaraniya, and I shall return within seven days for this reason." If a bhikkhu, having made such a resolve before departing, forgets it at the time of going, there is no fault. The teachers say that for one who goes without making any such resolve whatsoever, the Rains Residence is broken (vassaccheda). The teachers also state that for one who goes forth without any invitation or similar reason, but having resolved, "I shall return within a week," an offense occurs, but the Rains Residence is not broken (vassaccheda).

“Anto upacāra sīmāyaṃ ṭhiteneva sattāha karaṇīya nimittaṃ sallakkhetvā. Iminā nimittena gantvā anto sattāhe āgamissāmīti ābhogaṃ katvā gantuṃ vaṭṭati. Purimakkhaṇe ābhogaṃ katvā gamanakkhaṇe vissaritvā gatepi doso natthi. Sakaraṇīyo pakkamatīti vuttattā sabbathā pana ābhogaṃ akatvā gatassa vassacchedo ti vadanti. Yo pana sattāhakaraniya nimittābhāvepi sattāhabbhantare āgamissāmīti ābhogaṃ katvā gantvā, sattāhabbhantare āgacchati tassa āpattiyeva vassacchedo natthi. Sattāhassa sannivattattāti vadanti. Vīmaṃsitvā gahetabbaṃ” is a passage from the Vimativinodanī Ṭīkā.

A bhikkhu who has gone forth with a sattāhakaraniya must return to the place of Rains Residence before dawn on the seventh day, without exceeding seven days. If he departed from the boundary of the Rains Residence on a Sunday, he must return by Saturday, before the following Sunday dawns. If he remains outside until Sunday, the Rains Residence is broken (vassaccheda). A bhikkhu who has gone forth with a sattāhakaraniya is permitted not to exceed seven days outside, but to return within seven days. If the day of Pavāraṇā happens to be the seventh day, and a bhikkhu who has gone forth with a sattāhakaraniya performs Pavāraṇā somewhere else and does not return to the place of Rains Residence, the Rains Residence is not broken (vassaccheda). This is because for such a bhikkhu, the Rains period does not extend beyond the seventh day.

A Special Point

Pavāraṇā is a Monastic Procedure (vinaya-karma) appointed to be performed on that day, not an ending of the Rains Residence. Since the Uposatha day on which Pavāraṇā is performed also belongs to the Rains period, one should not depart from the place of Rains Residence on that day, thinking, "The Rains Residence has ended with Pavāraṇā." One must reside there on that day as well. Even on that day, if one departs from the place of Rains Residence having abandoned the intention to reside there, the Rains Residence may be broken (vassaccheda).
