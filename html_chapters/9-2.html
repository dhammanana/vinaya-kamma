Accessories Offered with the Kathina Robe

When a Kathina robe is offered, donors also offer many other requisites, such as robes, as accessory requisites. These are called ‘kaṭhinānisaṃsa parikkhāra’. The manner in which these should be dealt with is stated in the Vinaya commentary as follows.

Evam atthate pana kaṭhine, sace kaṭhina cīvarena saddhiṃ āhataṃ ānisaṃsaṃ dāyakā ‘yena amhākaṃ kaṭhinaṃ gahitaṃ tasseva demā’ti denti, bhikkhusaṅgho anissaro. Atha avicāretvāva datvā gacchanti, bhikkhusaṅgho issaro. Tasmā sace kaṭhinatthārakassa sesacīvarāni’pi dubbalāni honti. Saṅghena apaloketvā tesam’pi atthāya vatthāni dātabbāni, kammavācā pana ekāva vaṭṭati. Avasese kaṭhinānisaṃse balavavatthāni vassāvāsikaṭṭhitikāya dātabbāni. Ṭhitikāya abhāve therāsanato paṭṭhāya dātabbāni. Garubhāṇḍaṃ na bhājetabbaṃ.

Its meaning is as follows:-

‘When the Kathina has thus been spread, if the donors, bringing accessory requisites along with that Kathina robe, say, “We give these to the Elder who received our Kathina,” then they do not belong to the Community of monks. If the donors depart without saying anything about them, then those requisites belong to the Community. Therefore, if the other robes of the bhikkhu who received the Kathina are also worn out, then, having informed the Community, cloth should be given to the Kathina-spreader for those robes as well. The formal proclamation (kammavācā) is to be recited only for the Kathina robe. From the remaining Kathina-anisaṃsa, strong robes (sturdy, good robes) should be given according to the vaṭṭasāvāsikaṭṭhitikā. If there is no ṭṭhitikā, they should be given in order, starting from the senior-most Elder. Heavy articles (garubhāṇḍa) should not be divided.

In places where many bhikkhus reside, robes received by the Community are distributed in order of seniority as they are received. This is done as follows: If five robes are received today, they are given to five bhikkhus in order, starting from the senior-most Elder. The robes received thereafter are given in order, starting from the sixth Elder. “Ṭṭhitikā” refers to the point where the distribution of robes stopped. If there is a point where the distribution of robes, given in order, has stopped, then the Kathina-anisaṃsa robes are distributed starting from that ṭṭhitikā.
