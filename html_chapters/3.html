The Procedure for Higher Ordination.

The procedure for Higher Ordination is a grave Monastic Procedure in the Buddha's Dispensation. Unlike the act of going forth (pabbajja), it cannot be performed everywhere; it must be performed only within a consecrated boundary (sima). If it is performed in the Middle Country, it must be carried out by a Community of ten or more than ten bhikkhus, as stated: “Anujānāmi bhikkhave! dasavaggena vā atireka dasa vaggena vā gaṇena upasampādetuṃ”. If it is performed in the border districts, it may be carried out by a group of five bhikkhus with a Vinaya-expert as the fifth, as stated: “Anujānāmi bhikkhave! sabbapaccantimesu janapadesu vinayadharapañcamena gaṇena upasampādetuṃ”. The Blessed One Himself has designated the border districts.

They are as follows:-

“Puratthimāya disāya kajaṅgalaṃ nāma nigamo, tassā parena mahāsālā, tato’parā paccantimā janapadā, orato majjhe. Puratthimadakkhiṇāya disāya salalavatī nāma nadī, tato ‘Parā paccantimā janapadā, orato majjhe. Dakkhiṇāya disāya setakaṇṇikaṃ nāma nigamo, tato” parā paccantimā janapadā orato majjhe. Pacchimāya disāya thūnaṃ nāma b‍rāhmaṇa gāmo, tato’ parā paccantimā janapadā orato majjhe. Uttarāya disāya usīraddhajo nāma pabbato, tato’parā paccantimā janapadā orato majjhe.

Speaking from the perspective of the Middle Country: to the east, beyond the market town of Kajangala; to the south-east, beyond the river Salalavati; to the south, beyond the market town of Setakannika; to the west, beyond the brahmin village of Thuna; and to the north, beyond the mountain Usiraddhaja—these are the border districts. Since our country also belongs to the border districts, the procedure for Higher Ordination can be performed here with five bhikkhus. In performing a Higher Ordination, the majority of the duties fall upon the Act-proclaimer (kammacariya). When the candidate for Higher Ordination arrives at the boundary, if it is difficult to recite the Act-proclamation using the original names of the candidate and the Preceptor, the name Nāga should be assigned to the candidate and Tissa to the Preceptor. This authorization should be made by the Act-proclaimer as follows:

Discarding the former name of this candidate for Higher Ordination, I assign to him the name Nāga; and discarding the former name of the candidate's Preceptor, I assign to him the name Tissa for the ease of reciting the Act-proclamation. This should be known by the candidate for Higher Ordination, by the Preceptor, and by the Great Community.

After the authorization of names, the Act-proclaimer should speak to the candidate for Higher Ordination as follows:

Since the Protector of the World has stated, “Paṭhamaṃ upajjhaṃ gāhāpetabbo”: "O novice, who has come to receive Higher Ordination with the desire to cross the ocean of samsara and reach the city of Nibbana! You must first take a Preceptor."

When this is said, the candidate for Higher Ordination should sit in the squatting posture before the Preceptor and say the following sentence three times: “Upajjhāyo me bhante hohi”. The Preceptor should accept it by saying, “Pāsādikena sampādehi”.

Thereafter, the Act-proclaimer should speak as follows: "Venerable novice! Since the Blessed One, the Lord of the World, has stated, “Upajjhāṃ gāhāpetvā pattacīvaraṃ ācikkhitabbaṃ”, I shall now speak to you so that you may know the monastic designations regarding the bowl and robes. “Ayaṃ te patto”: Venerable novice, this black-colored, circular vessel is your bowl. “Ayaṃ saṃghāṭi”: This robe upon your shoulder, made of cut pieces, sewn double-layered, and dyed, is known in monastic usage as the outer robe (sanghati). “Ayaṃ uttarāsaṃgo”: This robe upon your body, made of cut pieces, sewn single-layered, and dyed, is known in monastic usage as the upper robe (uttarasanga). “Ayaṃ antaravāsako”: This robe which you are wearing is known in monastic usage as the inner robe (antaravasaka).

‘Gaccha: amumhi okāse tiṭṭhāhi. Venerable novice! Since the Tathagata has stated, “Anujānāmi bhikkhave! ekamantaṃ anusāsitvā saṅgha majjhe antarāyike dhamme pucchituṃ”—that the candidate for Higher Ordination should be instructed in a place apart from the Community, and since it is inappropriate to instruct you in the midst of the Community—go and stand in that place over there."

When this is said, the candidate for Higher Ordination should rise and, without turning his back to the Great Community, walk a short distance with hands joined in reverence and stand there. The Act-proclaimer should go there and instruct the candidate for Higher Ordination. Since it is stated, “Na bhikkhave! asammatena anusāsitabbo. Yo anusāsey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa”, one should not go to instruct without obtaining authorization. Now, the Act-proclaimer should authorize himself by reciting the following sentence:

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, nāgo āyasmato tissassa upasampadāpekkho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ ahaṃ nāgaṃ anusāsey‍yaṃ.

Thereafter, rising from the midst of the Community and approaching the candidate for Higher Ordination, he should instruct him while standing as follows:

Suṇasi nāga! aya te saccakālo, bhūtakālo, yaṃ jātaṃ taṃ saṅghamajjhe pucchante santaṃ atthiti vattabbaṃ. Asantaṃ natthiti vattabbaṃ. Mā kho vit‍thāsi, mā kho maṅku ahosi.

"O novice named Naga, who has dedicated your life to the Buddha's Dispensation to cross the ocean of samsara! This is the time for you to receive the supreme Higher Ordination morality, characterized by higher virtue in this most rare Dispensation of the All-Knowing One. This is the time to speak only the truth honestly, without hiding anything. This is the time to declare things as they are, without attempting to conceal the facts. If there is any disease such as leprosy or boils on your body, when you are questioned in the midst of the Community, you must say 'It is' if it exists. You must say 'It is not' if it does not exist. When questioned in the midst of the Community, do not be terrified with the fear that they are questioning you to do you harm. Do not be displeased or frown, thinking they are questioning whether non-existent things exist in order to disparage you."

“Evaṃ taṃ pucchissanti, santi te evarūpā ābādhā, kuṭṭhaṃ, gaṇḍo, kilāso, soso, apamāro, manussosi, purisosi, bhujissosi, anaṇosi, nasi rājabhaṭo, anuññātosi mātā pitūhi, paripuṇṇavīsativassosi, paripuṇṇaṃ te pattacīvaraṃ, kiṃ nāmosi, ko nāmo te upajjhāyoti.”

"Venerable novice! Since the Tathagata has allowed the questioning of the thirteen obstructive factors from the candidate for Higher Ordination, stating, “Anujānāmi bhikkhave! upasampādentena terasa antarāyike dhamme pucchituṃ”, they will question you in the midst of the Community during this preliminary stage of the Higher Ordination as follows: Do you have any of the diseases known as leprosy, boils, eczema, consumption, or epilepsy? Are you a human being? Are you a man? Are you a free man? Are you without debt? Are you not in royal service? Have you been permitted by your parents? Are you a full twenty years of age? Are your bowl and robes complete? What is your name? What is your Preceptor's name? Thus they will question you in the midst of the Community."

Having instructed him thus, leave the candidate for Higher Ordination standing there, and the Act-proclaimer should return to the midst of the Community and say:

“Suṇātu me bhante saṃgho, nāgo āyasmato tissassa upasampadāpekkho anusiṭṭho so mayā yadi saṃghassa pattallaṃ nāgo āgacchey‍ya”

Having said this sentence and obtained permission from the Community, summon the candidate for Higher Ordination to the midst of the Community. Thereafter, since it is stated, “Na bhikkhave! ayācitena upasampādetabbo. Yo upasampādey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa”, have the candidate for Higher Ordination request the Higher Ordination. At this point, the candidate for Higher Ordination should wear the robe over one shoulder, pay homage at the feet of the bhikkhus, sit in the squatting posture with hands joined in reverence, and say:

“Saṃghaṃ bhante upasampadaṃ yācāmi, ullumpatu maṃ bhante saṅgho anukampaṃ upādāya. Dutiyampi bhante saṅghaṃ upasampadaṃ yācāmi, ullumpatu maṃ bhante saṅgho anukampaṃ upādāya. Tatiyampi bhante saṅghaṃ upasampadaṃ yācāmi, ullumpatu maṃ bhaṇte saṅgho anukampaṃ upādāya.” Thus he should request Higher Ordination.

Then, the Act-proclaimer, having obtained authorization to question the obstructive factors through the formal act: “Suṇātu me bhante saṅgho, ayaṃ nāgo āyasmato tissassa upasampadāpekkho yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ ahaṃ nāgaṃ antarāyike dhamme pucchey‍yaṃ”, should question the obstructive factors as follows:

Suṇasi nāga! ayaṃ te saccakālo, bhūtakālo, yaṃ jātaṃ taṃ pucchāmi. Santaṃ atthiti vattabbaṃ. Asantaṃ natthiti vattabbaṃ. Santi te evarūpā ābādhā kuṭṭhaṃ? (natthi bhante) Gaṇḍo? (natthi bhante) Kilāso? (natthi bhante) Soso? (natthi bhante) Apamāro? (natthi bhante) Manussosi? (āma bhante) Purisosi? (āma bhante) Bhujissosi? (āma bhante) Anaṇosi? (āma bhante) Nasi rājābhaṭo? (āma bhante) Anuññātosi mātāpituhi? (āma bhante) Paripuṇṇa vīsati vassosi? (āma bhante) Paripuṇṇaṃ te pattacīvaraṃ? (āma bhante) Kiṃ nāmosi? (Ahaṃ bhante nāgo nāma) Ko nāmo te upajjhāyo? (Upajjhāyo me bhante āyasmā tissatthero nāma) After questioning the obstructive factors, the Higher Ordination should be performed using the Act-proclamation consisting of a motion and three announcements (natticatuttha-kamma).
