Persons who should not be ordained, but whose ordination is valid if performed.

“Na bhikkhave! ananuññāto mātā pitūhi putto pabbājetabbo. Yo pabbājey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa” Since this is a Vinaya regulation, do not ordain a son who has not been permitted by his mother and father. Even if one who was previously ordained with parental permission has left the robes and returns to be ordained again, do not ordain him without obtaining parental permission once more. If parents have entrusted a child saying, “I entrust him to you; ordain him whenever you wish,” then you may ordain that child whenever he comes, even if he has left the robes many times, without further consulting the parents. If someone comes to the monastery after a quarrel with his parents and asks for ordination, and when told to bring parental permission, says, “I will not go to my parents; if you do not ordain me, I will take my life or cause harm to you,” then ordain him for the sake of his protection. If his parents come and inquire about such a person who has been ordained, inform them of the circumstances.

“Na bhikkhave! pañcahi ābādhehi puṭṭho pabbājetabbo. Yo pabbājey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa” Since this is a Vinaya regulation, do not ordain anyone afflicted with any of the five diseases. The five diseases are those known as Kuṭṭha, gaṇḍa, kilāsa, sosa, apamāra.

‘Kuṭṭha’ (Leprosy) refers to diseases arising on the skin such as red spots, black spots, and scabs. Do not ordain one who has leprosy even the size of a little finger-nail if it is in the spreading stage. It is suitable to ordain one who has a small patch of leprosy only the size of a nail on a part of the body covered by the robe, provided it is in the healing stage. When ordaining a leper after treatment, ordain him only when the skin has returned to its natural color. The Vinaya Commentary states that one whose body is covered in ash-like scales like the back of a monitor lizard should also not be ordained.

‘Gaṇḍa’ (Boils) refers to unripened lumps arising here and there on the body like ebony fruits or limes. Some call these cystic tumors. Do not ordain one who has a growing boil even the size of a jujube fruit. One who has a non-growing boil the size of a jujube fruit in a covered area may be ordained. Do not ordain one who has non-growing boils on uncovered areas like the face. When ordaining after treating boils, ordain only after the skin has returned to normal. Do not ordain while the wound is still present.

‘Kilāsa’ (Eczema/Dry Leprosy) is a specific type of dry skin disease appearing in the color of red or white lotus petals. The condition commonly known today as vitiligo is Kilāsa. Regarding those with this disease, follow the same procedure as mentioned for leprosy.

‘Sosa’ (Consumption) is tuberculosis, which gradually wastes and dries the body day by day, leading the patient to death.

‘Apamāra’ (Epilepsy) is the falling sickness where a person suddenly loses consciousness and falls to the ground, sometimes struggling and foaming at the mouth with phlegm and blood. It is said to be a disease caused by non-human spirits.

“Na bhikkhave! rājabhaṭo pabbājetabbo. Yo pabbājey‍ya, āpatti dukkaṭassa” Since this is a Vinaya regulation, do not ordain a king’s soldier (government servant). A ‘king’s soldier’ is one who receives a salary from a king or a government for performing any task. It is proper to ordain such persons after they have resigned from their service.

“Na bhikkhave! dhajabandho coro pabbājetabbo. Yo pabbājey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa” As declared, do not ordain a notorious thief who commits serious robberies. One who was previously a notorious thief but has since abandoned that path and entered the right way, and is known as such by the public, is suitable for ordination. One who has committed minor thefts but is not publicly known as a thief may be given ordination.

“Na bhikkhave! kārabhedako coro pabbājetabbo. Yo pabbājey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa” Since these are Vinaya rules, do not ordain a thief who has escaped from confinement. This includes one who has escaped from a prison, one who has escaped from guards while being moved from place to place, and one who has fled an area after being restricted to it; all such persons are unfit for ordination. If one who was living righteously was imprisoned due to the slander of others and escaped, do not ordain him in that same region. He may be ordained in another region.

“Na bhikkhave! likhitako coro pabbājetabbo. Yo pabbājey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa” Since these are Vinaya rules, do not ordain a thief whose name is recorded by the government for arrest or punishment.

“Na bhikkhave! kasāhato katadaṇḍakammo pabbājetabbo. Yo pabbājey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa” Since these are Vinaya rules, do not ordain one who has been flogged with a whip or other instrument as punishment. One who was beaten for failing to follow instructions may be ordained. Those who should not be ordained are those flogged as punishment for non-payment of taxes, non-payment of debts, etc. Even such a person is fit to receive ordination after the wounds have healed.

“Na bhikkhave! lakkhaṇāhato katadaṇḍakammo pabbājetabbo. Yo pabbājey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa” Since these are Vinaya rules, do not ordain one who has been branded or marked on the body as punishment. If the branded person is a free man, he may be ordained after the wounds have healed and the scars have faded. One who has scars in places not visible when the robes are worn covering the three circles may also be ordained.

“Na bhikkhave! iṇāyiko pabbājetabbo. Yo pabbājey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa” As declared, do not ordain debtors. If a relative or a lay supporter of the person seeking ordination undertakes to pay the debt, he may be ordained. If the preceptor has funds to pay the debt, he may ordain the debtor with the intention of freeing him by paying the creditors. Do not ordain with the thought of finding money to pay the debt later. There is no offense for the preceptor if he ordains a person without knowing he is a debtor.

“Na bhikkhave! dāso pabbājetabbo. Yo pabbājey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa” As declared, do not ordain a slave. Since there are no slaves at present, no detailed explanation is provided here. There is an extensive explanation regarding this in the Commentary.

Thirty-two types of persons who should not be ordained.

“Na bhikkhave! hatthacchinno pabbājetabbo. Na pādacchinno pabbājetabbo. Na hatthapādacchinno pabbājetabbo. Na kaṇṇacchinno pabbājetabbo. Na kāsacchinno pabbājetabbo. Nakaṇṇanāsacchinno pabbājetabbo. Na aṅgulicchinno pabbājetabbo. Na alacchinno pabbājetabbo. Na kaṇḍaracchinno pabbājetabbo. Na phaṇahattho pabbājetabbo. Na khujjo pabbājetabbo. Na vāmano pabbājetabbo. Na galagaṇḍi pabbājetabbo. Na lakkhaṇāhato pabbājetabbo. Na kasāhato pabbājetabbo. Na likhitako pabbājetabbo. Na sīpadi pabbājetabbo. Na pāparogi pabbājetabbo. Na parisadūsako pabbājetabbo. Na kāṇo pabbājetabbo. Na kuṇi pabbājetabbo. Na bañjo pabbājetabbo. Na pakkhahato pabbājetabbo. Na chinniriyāpatho pabbājetabbo. Na jarādubbalo pabbājetabbo. Na andho pabbājetabbo. Na mūgo pabbājetabbo. Na badhiro pabbājetabbo. Na andhamūgo pabbājetabbo. Na andhabadhiro pabbājetabbo. Na mugabadhiro pabbājetabbo. Na andhamugabadhiro pabbājetabbo. Yo pabbājey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa.

As the Tathāgata has declared in the Vinaya, the following should not be ordained: one whose hands are cut off, one whose feet are cut off, one whose hands and feet are cut off, one whose ears are cut off, one whose nose is cut off, one whose ears and nose are cut off, one whose thumbs are cut off, one whose little fingers are cut off, one with severed tendons, one with webbed fingers, a hunchback, a dwarf, one with a goiter, one with branded marks on the body, one with marks of flogging, one who is a wanted criminal, one with elephantiasis, one with a bad disease, one who disfigures the assembly, one who is one-eyed, one with a crooked limb, one who is lame, one who is paralyzed on one side, a cripple, one feeble with age, one who is blind, one who is dumb, one who is deaf, one who is blind and dumb, one who is blind and deaf, one who is dumb and deaf, and one who is blind, dumb, and deaf. If ordained, there is an offense of wrong-doing for the preceptor.

One whose finger-tips only are cut off such that even a small portion of the nail remains may be ordained. If even one finger is cut off such that no nail remains, he should not be ordained.

‘One with severed tendons’ refers to one who, due to severed tendons, cannot place the entire sole of the foot on the ground and walks only on the tips of the toes or only on the heels.

‘One with webbed fingers’ refers to one whose fingers are joined together.

‘One with a bad disease’ refers to one suffering from diseases such as asthma, hemorrhoids, fistula, or chronic cough, which are loathed by the public.

‘One who disfigures the assembly’ refers to one who, due to deformity, makes the assembly look unsightly. This includes: extremely tall persons whose heads reach the waist of others; extremely short persons like monstrous images; extremely dark persons like a charred log; extremely pale persons like a plastered wall; extremely thin persons like hungry ghosts (petas) with only skin and bone; extremely obese persons who find it difficult to move; those with massive bellies like goblins (pisacas); and those with heads so large they cannot be held up. A detailed description of those who disfigure the assembly is found in the Vinaya Commentary, where many such types are listed.
