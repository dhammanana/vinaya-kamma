The Nine Uposathas.

There are three Uposathas according to the days: the Cātuddasī Uposatha, the Paṇṇarasī Uposatha, and the Sāmaggī Uposatha.

There are three Uposathas according to the individuals performing the act: the Saṅgha Uposatha, the Gaṇa Uposatha, and the Puggala Uposatha.

There are three Uposathas according to the manner of performance: the Suttuddesa Uposatha, the Pārisuddhi Uposatha, and the Adhiṭṭhāna Uposatha. Thus, there are nine Uposathas.

Among the nine kinds of Uposathas, the "Cātuddasī Uposatha" is the Uposatha performed on the fourteenth day of the fortnight. It is performed in the third and seventh fortnights of the seasons (which do not have fifteen days). The 'Paṇṇarasī Uposatha' is the Uposatha performed on the fifteenth day of the fortnight. The 'Sāmaggī Uposatha' is the Uposatha performed on the day the divided Community of monks was reconciled, commemorating that reconciliation. The 'Saṅgha Uposatha' is the Uposatha performed by four or more bhikkhus. The 'Gaṇa Uposatha' is the Uposatha performed by three bhikkhus. The 'Puggala Uposatha' is the Uposatha performed by two bhikkhus or one bhikkhu. The 'Suttuddesa Uposatha' is the Uposatha performed by way of reciting the Pātimokkha. The 'Pārisuddhi Uposatha' is the Uposatha performed by way of declaring one's purity.
