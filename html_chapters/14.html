The Act of Assigning a Site for a Hut

The bhikkhu building the hut should clear the site for the hut, approach the Community, arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, pay homage at the feet of the senior bhikkhus, sit on his heels, raise his joined palms, and request the Community three times as follows:

“Ahaṃ bhante! saññācikāya kuṭiṃ kattukāmo assāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. So’haṃ bhante! saṅghaṃ kuṭivatthuolokanaṃ yācāmi.”

When the bhikkhu building the hut has thus requested, if the Community wishes to go and inspect the site, it is well. If not, the Community should authorize competent bhikkhus who are capable of discerning whether there are obstructions or a lack of open space, and send them to inspect the site.

The Formal Act of Authorization.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (tisso) bhikkhu saññācikāya kuṭiṃ kattukāmo assāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. So saṅghaṃ kuṭivat‍thu olokanaṃ yācati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (buddharakkhitañca dhammarakkhitañca) bhikkhu sammanney‍ya (tissassa) bhikkhuno kuṭivat‍thuṃ oloketaṃ: esā ñatti.

Saṅgho (buddharakkhitañca dhammarakkhitañca) bhikkhu sammannati (tissassa) bhikkhuno kuṭivat‍thuṃ oloketuṃ. Yassāyasmato khamati (buddharakkhitassa ca dhammarakkhitassa ca) bhikkhūnaṃ sammuti (tissassa) bhikkhuno kuṭivat‍thuṃ oloketuṃ, so tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati, so bhāsey‍ya.

Sammatā saṅghena (buddharakkhito ca dhammarakkhito ca) bhikkhu (tissassa) bhikkhuno kuṭivat‍thu oloketuṃ, khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī, evametaṃ dhārayāmi.

The authorized bhikkhus should go to the site where the hut is to be built and investigate whether it is a place with obstructions, and whether there is enough open space for a cart yoked with two oxen to pass around the hut. If it is a place with obstructions or lacks the required open space, they should inform the bhikkhu building the hut that he should not build there. If the site is suitable and appropriate for building a hut, they should inform the Community. Thereafter, the bhikkhu building the hut should approach the Community, pay homage at the feet of the senior bhikkhus, sit on his heels, raise his joined palms, and request the assignment of the site for the hut.

The Formal Act for Assigning the Site for a Hut.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (tisso) bhikkhu saññācikāya kuṭiṃ kattukāmo assāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ, so saṅghaṃ kuṭivat‍thudesanaṃ yācati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (tissassa) bhikkhuno kuṭivat‍thu desey‍ya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (tisso) bhikkhu saññācikāya kuṭiṃ kattukāmo assāmikaṃ attuddesaṃ. So saṅghaṃ kuṭivat‍thu desanaṃ yācati. Saṅgho (tissassa) bhikkhuno kuṭivat‍tuṃ deseti. Yassāyasmato khamati (tissassa) bhikkhuno kuṭivat‍thu desanā, so tuṇhassa, yassa nakkhamati so bhāsey‍ya.

Desitaṃ saṅghena (tissassa) bhikkhuno kuṭivat‍thu. Khamati saṅghassa. Tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.

The purpose of having the site assigned is to obtain the formal approval of the Community to build a hut or a dwelling. A bhikkhu who builds a hut for his own use with materials he himself has begged for, without having the site assigned, commits an offense of Sanghadisesa.

The Assignment of a Site for a Large Dwelling.

A bhikkhu who is building or having a large dwelling built with a benefactor must also have the site assigned by the Community before proceeding. If he builds the dwelling without doing so, he commits an offense of Sanghadisesa.
