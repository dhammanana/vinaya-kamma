Pavāraṇā

Pavāraṇā is twofold: 'rejection' and 'invitation'. The Pavāraṇā mentioned in this training rule: “yo pana bhikkhu bhuttāvi pavārito anatirittaṃ khādanīyaṃ vā bhojanīyaṃ vā khādeyya vā bhuñjeyya vā pācittiyaṃ” is rejection. This Pavāraṇā, which is to be performed on the Uposatha day concluding the Rains-residence, is not a rejection like the Pavāraṇā of lay devotees observing precepts. The Pavāraṇā intended here is the request made to the Community of monks, or to a group, or to an individual, to admonish and instruct oneself concerning any fault seen, heard, or suspected from these three causes. This should not be mistaken for the termination of the Rains-residence.

Pavāraṇā, according to the days, is threefold: Cātuddasī Pavāraṇā, Paṇṇarasī Pavāraṇā, and Sāmaggī Pavāraṇā.

According to the performing individuals, it is threefold: Sangha Pavāraṇā, Gaṇa Pavāraṇā, and Puggala Pavāraṇā.

According to the manner of performance, it is threefold: Tevācika Pavāraṇā, Dvevācika Pavāraṇā, and Ekavācika Pavāraṇā. Thus, Pavāraṇā is ninefold.

The Uposatha day concluding the Rains-residence is always a Paṇṇarasī day, as it is the sixth Uposatha of the rainy season. Mostly, Pavāraṇā is performed on that day. If there is a need, the Community of monks may postpone the Pavāraṇā from that day to the seventh or eighth Uposatha day of the rainy season. A Cātuddasī Pavāraṇā occurs if the Pavāraṇā is performed on the seventh Uposatha day of the rainy season. The Pavāraṇā performed on the eighth Uposatha day of the rainy season is also a Paṇṇarasī Pavāraṇā.

If a suitable reason for a Sāmaggī Uposatha arises within the twenty-nine days from the new moon of Vap month until the full moon of Il month, a Sāmaggī Pavāraṇā should be performed. It is not performed at any other time. For a Sangha Pavāraṇā, four bhikkhus are not sufficient, just as for a Sangha Uposatha. At least five bhikkhus must assemble for a Sangha Pavāraṇā to be performed.
