How to Proceed Regarding Unlawful Procedures

The group-of-six bhikkhus began performing unlawful procedures in the midst of the Community of monks. The bhikkhus reported this matter to the Blessed One. Thereupon, the Tathāgata, saying, “Na bhikkhave adhammakammaṃ kātabbaṃ, yo karey‍ya āpatti dukkaṭassa”, laid down an offense of wrong-doing for those bhikkhus who perform unlawful procedures. Even then, without ceasing, those bhikkhus continued to perform unlawful procedures. The bhikkhus reported this to the Blessed One. Then the Tathāgata, by saying, “Anujānāmi bhikkhave adhammakamme kayiramāne paṭikkosituṃ”, authorized the bhikkhus assembled there to protest and prevent the unlawful procedure. When they did so, the group-of-six bhikkhus, being angry, threatened those bhikkhus. When this was reported to the Blessed One, by saying, “Anujānāmi bhikkhave diṭṭhimpi āvikātuṃ”, he authorized them to declare their view, saying, "This is an unlawful procedure; this is not acceptable to us."

When the group-of-six bhikkhus were performing unlawful procedures, the bhikkhus told the group-of-six bhikkhus themselves that it was not acceptable to them. Angered by this, the group-of-six bhikkhus threatened those bhikkhus. When this was reported to the Blessed One, by saying, Anujānāmi bhikkhave catūhi pañcahi paṭikkosituṃ, dvīhi tīhi diṭṭhiṃ āvikātuṃ, ekena adhiṭṭhātuṃ, he authorized four or five bhikkhus to protest, two or three to declare their view, and a single bhikkhu to make a mental resolution.

When the bhikkhu reciting the motion says, “Yassa nakkhamati so bhāsey‍ya”, if it is an unlawful procedure, the bhikkhus who do not approve must declare their view, saying, “Adhammakammaṃ etaṃ, na me taṃ khamati”. If one is afraid to declare it so that all may hear, he should declare it to a bhikkhu staying near him. If even that is not possible, he should resolve, “Na me taṃ khamati”.

If a bhikkhu who has arrived within the boundary to participate in the procedure protests, or at the very least expresses his disapproval to a bhikkhu near him, that procedure is not a valid procedure. A failure of the procedure occurs only if the opposition is expressed within the boundary, not elsewhere afterwards. The protest of unordained persons, those of different communion, ordained insane persons, bhikkhus suffering from such intense pain that they lose consciousness, or bhikkhus outside the boundary does not render the procedure invalid.

Here ends the book on Monastic Procedures compiled by the Venerable Acariya Rerukane Chandavimala Mahasthavira.
