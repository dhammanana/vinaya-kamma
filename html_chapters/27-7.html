Authorization of a Robe-Distributor.

Robes received by the Community are stored in the storehouse to be distributed when a sufficient quantity has accumulated. When the robes in the storehouse became numerous, the bhikkhus informed the Tathagata. At that time, the Tathagata allowed distribution by the Community assembled within the precinct, saying: “Anujānāmi bhikkhave! sammukhībhūtena saṅghena bhājetuṃ”. During the distribution, there was much noise, with some saying, "Give to our preceptors," or "Give to our teachers," and so forth. When this was reported to the Blessed One, He allowed the authorization of a bhikkhu endowed with five qualities to distribute the robes. The five qualities of a Robe-Distributor are: that he does not follow a wrong course through favoritism, through hatred, through delusion, or through fear; and that he knows what has been distributed and what has not.

The Procedural Text for Authorization.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ cīvarabhājakaṃ sammanney‍ya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, saṅgho (tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ cīvarabhājakaṃ sammannati. Yassāyasmato khamati (tissassa) bhikkhuno cīvarabhājakassa sammuti. So tuṇhassa. Yassa nikkhamati, so bhāsey‍ya.

Sammato saṅghena (tisso) bhikkhu cīvarabhājako. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.

The Robe-Distributor bhikkhus wondered, "How should the robes be distributed?" When this was reported to the Tathagata, He said: “Anujānāmi bhikkhave! paṭhamaṃ uccinit‍vā tulayit‍vā vaṇṇāvaṇṇaṃ kat‍vā bhikkhu gaṇetvā vaggaṃ bandhitvā cīvarapaṭiviṃsaṃ ṭhapetuṃ” — "Bhikkhus, I allow you first to sort the robes, weigh their value, equalize the value by adding items to those of lesser value, count the bhikkhus, form them into groups, and set aside the robe-shares." Among the robes offered individually by many people, there are those of good and bad quality, and higher and lower value. Therefore, one should examine the robes and equalize the shares by adding other robes to those of lesser value. Bhikkhus should be grouped for distribution if their number is so large that individual distribution becomes difficult. If there are many bhikkhus, they should be grouped into tens, or fours or fives; robe-shares should be set aside according to the number of groups, and distributed by drawing lots. The bhikkhus within each group should then further divide the shares among themselves by drawing lots. This is the method for distributing robes.
