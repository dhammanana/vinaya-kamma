Three-Time Sangha Pavāraṇā

After the Community has assembled within the sīmā, one bhikkhu shall obtain the Authorization for the Requestor with the statement “Suṇātu me bhante saṅgho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ. Ahaṃ āyasmantaṃ (tissaṃ) pavāraṇā ñattiṭhapanaṃ ajjhesey‍yaṃ”, and then, saying “Saṅgho bhante āyasmantaṃ (tissaṃ) pavāraṇā ñattiṭhapanaṃ ajjhesati. Ṭhapetu bhante āyasmā (tisso) pavāraṇā ñattiṃ” three times, invite the Sangha Thera or another learned elder to lay down the Pavāraṇā Motion.

Thereupon, the invited elder shall reply “Āma ṭhapemi āvuso” and, saying the statement “Suṇātu me bhante saṅgho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ ahaṃ āyasmantaṃ (nāgaṃ) vinayaṭa pucchey‍yaṃ”, lay down the Motion for questioning on Monastic Procedures.

The bhikkhu who made the invitation shall lay down the Motion for answering on Monastic Procedures with “Suṇātu me bhante saṅgho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ ahaṃ āyasmatā (tissena) vinayaṭa puṭṭho vissajjey‍yaṃ”. Thereupon, the questioning and answering on Monastic Procedures shall be carried out as follows.

Question:-

“Sammajjanī padīpo ca - udakaṃ āsanena ca

Pavāraṇāya etāni - pubbakaraṇanti vuccati”

As declared by the Commentarial Masters, are these four preliminary duties—sweeping, lighting lamps, providing drinking water and water for use, and arranging seats—which must be done before the Community assembles for the Pavāraṇā, completed?

Answer:- As there is sunlight now, lighting lamps is unnecessary. The rest are completed.

Q: “Chandapavāraṇā utukkhānaṃ - bhikkhu gaṇanā ca ovādo

pavāraṇā ya etāni - pubbakiccanti vuccati”.

As declared by the Commentarial Masters, have the brought-in declarations of consent (chandapavāraṇā) been made known to the Community, and are they completed?

A: As all bhikkhus within the sīmā are assembled here, maintaining physical presence without deficiency, there is no need to bring in declarations of purity (chandapārisuddhi).

Q: The seasons are threefold: winter, summer, and rainy season. Among these seasons, which season has now arrived?

A: The season that has now arrived is the rainy season.

Q: Each season has eight fortnights. How many fortnights have passed in this current rainy season? How many fortnights remain? Which fortnight is this current fortnight?

A: Five fortnights have passed in this rainy season. Two fortnights remain. The current fortnight is the sixth fortnight.

Q: What is the number of bhikkhus now assembled within this sīmā?

Provide the answer.

Q: Has the request made by the bhikkhunīs for receiving admonition been made known to the Community and completed?

A: As there are no bhikkhunīs at this time, there is no such request.

Q:

“Pavāraṇā yāvatikā ca bhikkhu

kammappattā sabhāgāpattiyo ca

na vijjanti vajjanīyā ca puggalā

tasmiṃ na honti pattakallanti vuccati”

As declared by the Commentarial Masters, although Pavāraṇā is generally one, it is ninefold in terms of specific characteristics: three by day, namely Cātuddasī, Paṇṇarasī, Sāmaggī; three by performing individuals, namely Saṅgha, Gaṇa, Puggala; and three by mode of performance, namely Tevācika, Dvevācika, Ekavācika. Which Pavāraṇā, by day, is this Pavāraṇā that is now to be performed?

A:

It is the Paṇṇarasī Pavāraṇā.

Q:

Which Pavāraṇā, by performing individuals, is it?

A:

It is the Saṅgha Pavāraṇā.

Q:

Which Pavāraṇā, by mode of performance, is it?

A:

It is the Tevācika Pavāraṇā.

Q:

When it is said Tevācika Pavāraṇā, a minimum of five bhikkhus or more are required for it. Are that many bhikkhus now assembled here?

A:

Yes, they are.

Q:

Are all bhikkhus free from similar offenses (sabhāgāpatti)?

A:

The bhikkhus have purified themselves by confessing their offenses to one another.

Q:

Have the twenty-one objectionable individuals been excluded from the Community's presence and is this completed?

A:

Yes, they have.

Thereupon, the elder who questions on Monastic Procedures shall say: “Pubbakaraṇa pubbakiccāni samāpetvā desitāpattikassa samaggassa bhikkhusaṅghassa anumatiyā pavāraṇā ñattiṃ ṭhapetuṃ ārādhanā karoma”. The bhikkhus assembled shall respond to this by giving their assent (sādhukāra).
