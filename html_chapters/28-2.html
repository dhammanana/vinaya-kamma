Purification of the Boundary Area.

When a new boundary is to be delimited, purify the ground as follows: Level and clean the area of ground to be taken for the boundary delimitation and a space of about two fathoms outside it on all four sides, and divide it into bed-sized plots. While doing this, perform it cleanly by driving stakes and pulling ropes to draw lines, or by drawing lines with lime or similar substances, or by driving stakes at all four corners of every plot. Thereafter, a group of four, five, or six bhikkhus should perform the act of boundary removal in order, standing in all the plots. While doing this, it is better if all bhikkhus who are not participating in the act are removed from the village, thus performing the purification of the village as well. If the village cannot be purified, remove other bhikkhus to a distance of about twenty or twenty-five fathoms nearby and perform the act of removal.

Since it is difficult to understand the suitability or unsuitability of the bhikkhus participating in the act, it is good for the sake of purification and to remove doubts regarding the boundary to have different groups of bhikkhus perform the act of removal two or three times. When performing this act, some bhikkhus who do not know the Monastic Discipline may wish to come near to watch the act being performed. If bhikkhus come near, they must be prevented. The 'bed-size' is a measure of five cubits in length and two and a half cubits in width. When the Community stands in all such divided chambers in any ground and performs the act of removal, if any part of an ancient boundary was caught in that ground, that boundary is removed and the ground becomes purified.

This removal is performed to purify a new boundary ground where it is not known where an existing boundary might be. A boundary removal occurs only if the group of bhikkhus performing the act stands within the boundary being removed while performing the act. If even one of the four bhikkhus performing the act of removal stands outside the boundary ground, the removal does not take place. Divide the area into chambers and perform the boundary removal while standing everywhere so that wherever a boundary might have been, it will be removed.

Sometimes a small portion of an ancient boundary, not large enough for four bhikkhus to stand in, might be caught in the ground where the new boundary is being delimited. By performing the act of removal only in the ground where the new boundary is being delimited, that boundary containing the small portion is not removed. This is because the Community did not stand within it to perform the removal. Therefore, perform the act of removal in the ground outside the new boundary for at least a distance of two fathoms. Do not allow other bhikkhus to come near the act of removal because if other bhikkhus are inside the ancient boundary being removed while the act is being performed, the removal will not take place.

“Sīmā bhikkhave! samuhanantena paṭhamaṃ ticīvarena avippavāso samuhantabbo. Pacchā samāna saṃvāsā simā samuhan tabbā” According to this rule of Monastic Discipline, when removing a boundary, first perform the removal of the boundary of non-separation from the three robes with the removal formula. Thereafter, perform the removal of the boundary of common communion with the removal formula. Since the boundary of common communion and the boundary of non-separation from the three robes exist on the same ground, if they are not removed in both ways, the purification of the ground does not occur.

After the act of removal is performed systematically as stated, if there were ancient boundaries caught in that ground, all of them disappear and that ground becomes a purified village boundary. All Monastic Procedures can be performed in a village boundary as well. Boundary delimitation is also done within a village boundary. While the village itself is a suitable boundary for all Monastic Procedures, delimiting another boundary within it is for the convenience of performing Monastic Procedures. If a Monastic Procedure is to be performed in a village boundary, all bhikkhus residing in the village must be gathered there. The consent of bhikkhus who cannot attend must be obtained. While performing a Monastic Procedure in this way, if a bhikkhu happens to arrive anywhere in that village, it results in the failure of the act. Because of these facts, it is not easy to frequently perform Monastic Procedures in village boundaries. When a small boundary is delimited, only the bhikkhus residing within that boundary need to be gathered for a Monastic Procedure. Even if many bhikkhus are standing outside the boundary in plain sight, it does not harm the acts performed within the boundary. Small boundaries are delimited for that purpose.

After systematically removing the boundary and purifying the ground, if the boundary is to be delimited using stone marks, place adequate stone pillars in the four directions or the eight directions. If the boundary is to be delimited using water marks, dig holes in the four or eight directions and fill them with water. There is no fault in setting up the stone pillars before purifying the ground. At the time of delimiting the boundary within the prepared marks, bring all fellow bhikkhus living in that village to that boundary ground. If there are other bhikkhus living in that village who do not come there, their consent must be obtained. Or, make the bhikkhus who do not come there at the time of the act stay outside that village.

Place guards on the roads to stop guest bhikkhus who might enter the village unexpectedly. If a bhikkhu travels even in a vehicle on a road through the village while the act of boundary delimitation is being performed, the boundary delimitation fails. After guarding the village and the Community has gathered at the place of boundary delimitation, first proclaim the marks of the boundary being delimited so the Community can hear. This should be done by the bhikkhu who recites the formal act. The bhikkhu reciting the formal act should stand up, go to the eastern side of the boundary ground, and ask: “Puratthimāya disāya kiṃ nimittaṃ?” ('What is the mark in the eastern direction?'). If the mark is a stone pillar, another should answer: “Pāsāṇo bhante” ('A stone, venerable sir'). There is no fault even if the answer is given by an unordained person. Thereafter, the expert in Monastic Discipline should proclaim the mark so the Community can hear: “Phaso pāsāṇo nimittaṃ” ('This stone is the mark').

Thereafter, go to the south-east and ask: “Puratthimāya anudisāya kiṃ nimittaṃ?” If the mark there is a stone pillar, the answer “Pāsāṇo bhante” should be given. Thereafter, the expert in Monastic Discipline should proclaim the mark: “Phaso pāsāṇo nimittaṃ”. In this way, after asking and proclaiming the marks in all eight directions in order, to connect mark to mark, go back to the east and ask and proclaim the mark as before. Proclaiming the marks once in this way is sufficient. However, it is better if it is done three times.

When proclaiming the marks, if there are many marks in one direction, ask the first mark: “Puratthimāya disāya kiṃ nimittaṃ?” and then proclaim the remaining marks by asking: “Purat‍thimāya disāya aparaṃ kiṃ nimittaṃ” as long as there are marks. The same should be followed for the remaining directions. After proclaiming the marks, as it was said: “Sīmaṃ bhikkhave sammannantena paṭhamaṃ samānasaṃvāsā sīmā sammannitabbā pacchā ticīvarena avippavāso sammannitabbo”, when granting the boundary authorization, first perform the authorization of the boundary of common communion. Afterwards, perform the authorization of non-separation from the three robes on that same ground as appropriate. Immediately upon finishing the recitation of the boundary delimitation formula, such as “Suṇātu me bhante saṅgho”, according to the Buddha's command without error, the ground within the proclaimed marks becomes the boundary ground suitable for the Monastic Procedures of the Community, extending down to the water that supports the earth.

A ground where the boundary has been delimited once in this way will remain as a boundary as long as the Dispensation of the All-Knowing One lasts, unless the Community removes it again. Only the Community can make that ground a non-boundary again. Even if the boundary ground is raised later by filling it with soil, that ground remains the boundary. Even if a river flows over the boundary ground, pillars can be set up there, a platform built, and Monastic Procedures performed.

If two boundaries are delimited as a Major Boundary and a Section Boundary, an empty space must be left between those two boundaries. Stone pillars should also be placed outside the marks of the Section Boundary so that the boundary interval is clearly visible. Since Major Boundary delimitation is not performed nowadays, the method of delimiting boundaries as Section Boundaries and Major Boundaries has not been included in this book.
