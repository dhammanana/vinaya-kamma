Monks who Receive and Do Not Receive the Kathina Spreading

“Kaṭhinatthāraṃ ke labhanti? Ke na labhanti? Gaṇanavasena tāva pacchimakoṭiyā pañcajanā labhanti. Uddhaṃ satasahassampi pañcannaṃ heṭṭhā na labhanti. Vutthavassavasena purimikāya vassaṃ upagantvā paṭhamapavāraṇāya pavāritā labhanti. Jinnavassā vā pacchimikāya upagatā vā na labhanti.”

According to what is stated in the Vinaya Commentary, for the performance of the Kathina spreading Monastic Procedure, there must be at least five bhikkhus. The Kathina is spread by one bhikkhu. This can only be done with a robe given by the Community of monks to a bhikkhu. Therefore, apart from the bhikkhu who receives it, four other bhikkhus must be present to give the robe. That is why there must be at least five bhikkhus for a Kathina to be spread. The Kathina can only be spread by a bhikkhu who observed the Rains Retreat previously and performed the Pavarana at the first Pavarana. Any bhikkhu may assist in assigning the Kathina cloth. Only bhikkhus who observed the Rains Retreat previously and performed the Pavarana receive the benefits of the Kathina. Others do not receive them. Bhikkhus who observed the Rains Retreat and performed the Pavarana in another monastery also do not receive the benefits of a Kathina spread in one monastery. If there is even one bhikkhu who observed the Rains Retreat previously and performed the Pavarana in a certain place, that bhikkhu can spread the Kathina there with the assistance of other bhikkhus.
