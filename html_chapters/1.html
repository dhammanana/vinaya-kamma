Preface

“Vinayo nāma sāsanassa āyu, vinaye ṭhite sāsanaṃ ṭhitaṃ hoti” — In accordance with these words of the Great Arahants who conducted the Recitations, the Vinaya Pitaka, being the teachings of the Tathagata’s authority, is the very life of the Dispensation. Since the Dispensation of the Buddha endures only as long as the Discipline endures, all bhikkhus devoted to the Dispensation must study the Vinaya Pitaka with the utmost reverence. The formal acts found in the Vinaya Pitaka, such as the establishment of boundaries and Higher Ordination, are called Ecclesiastical Acts (Vinaya Kamma). Since the entry of clansmen into the Buddha’s Dispensation through Higher Ordination, and the repeated purification of the virtue of those who have entered, must be accomplished through the path of Ecclesiastical Acts, these acts hold a supreme position in the Dispensation.

When performing Ecclesiastical Acts, they must be done with extreme purity, without deviating from the methods prescribed in the Vinaya. An Ecclesiastical Act performed incorrectly is as if it were not performed at all. Even if done through ignorance, performing an Ecclesiastical Act incorrectly is a grave evil; it leads to the destruction of the Buddha’s Dispensation. If a boundary is established incorrectly, even if stone pillars are erected, it is not a valid boundary. Not a single Ecclesiastical Act performed therein will be valid. Even if a Higher Ordination is conducted there, those being ordained do not become fully ordained (Upasampanna). In the future, any ordinations or Higher Ordinations conducted by those who were ordained in such an invalid boundary will also be invalid. Even if the boundary is valid, if the act of Higher Ordination itself is performed incorrectly, they remain unordained. The pupils ordained by them in the future will likewise remain unordained. The gravity of the harm caused by performing Ecclesiastical Acts incorrectly must be understood from these facts.

To gain thorough knowledge of Ecclesiastical Acts, one must master the entire Vinaya Pitaka. Otherwise, the Ecclesiastical Acts must be studied separately. Merely studying books is not sufficient to perform Ecclesiastical Acts correctly; one must also have practical experience in performing them. In the past, many bhikkhus possessed both knowledge and practical experience in Ecclesiastical Acts. It must be said that today, bhikkhus with thorough knowledge and practical experience are not common. There are several reasons for this.

The bhikkhu of the past could devote his entire time to the study of the Dhamma and Vinaya. The modern bhikkhu, in order to live with a certain status in the world, is compelled to learn many things besides the Dhamma and Vinaya. Therefore, the modern bhikkhu must spend the greater part of his period of study on subjects outside the Dhamma and Vinaya. This is one reason for the deficiency in knowledge of Dhamma and Vinaya among contemporary bhikkhus. Furthermore, a bhikkhu who returns to his monastery after completing his studies at a Pirivena must devote his entire time to temple affairs, the needs of the lay supporters, the business of the Community, and the work of providing for his own requirements. This too is a reason for the deficiency in knowledge of Dhamma and Vinaya.

Another reason why Sri Lankan bhikkhus are unpracticed in Ecclesiastical Acts is that the act of Higher Ordination in various Nikāyas has been monopolized by specific groups. The act of Higher Ordination was not intended by the Tathagata to be the exclusive right of only one group. It belongs to all bhikkhus who possess Higher Ordination. In any region outside the Middle Country (Majjhima-desa), five bhikkhus who possess Higher Ordination may assemble within a boundary and perform the act of Higher Ordination.

During the time of the Buddha, candidates for Higher Ordination in various regions were ordained by the bhikkhus residing in those very regions. Even now in Burma, the act of Higher Ordination in various regions is performed by the local bhikkhus. There is no specific group or specific place designated solely for the act of Higher Ordination. Higher Ordinations performed everywhere are accepted without distinction. Since the act of Higher Ordination is performed in all parts of the country, all bhikkhus gain the practice of performing Ecclesiastical Acts by having the opportunity to participate in them. If that custom were to prevail in Sri Lanka as well, many bhikkhus would naturally receive training in Ecclesiastical Acts.

One can speak of both the merits and demerits of the act of Higher Ordination being restricted to one group. Whatever merits it may have, there is also a most dangerous disadvantage. That is: if the group holding the right to ordain should somehow become an impure group, the Higher Ordination would be lost to the entire Nikāya—to the whole lineage of the Community in that Nikāya. The best way for a candidate to obtain Higher Ordination is to receive it from a group whom he knows well and can accept without doubt as possessing pure Higher Ordination. A bhikkhu who has obtained Higher Ordination in this way will have no doubt regarding his own status. That lack of doubt becomes a reason for him to guard his virtue with devotion. One who receives Higher Ordination from an unknown group whose details are not known will not have firm confidence in his own Higher Ordination. Often, doubts (kukkucca) arise regarding the ordination. This is a great obstacle and a cause for discouragement for a clansman who desires to fulfill the monastic life well.

Another disadvantage of the act of Higher Ordination being restricted to one group is that candidates and their teachers are forced to provide a large amount of money. As things stand today, Higher Ordination cannot be obtained without spending money. The Tathagata, who laid down training rules prohibiting the use of money by monastics, would not have prescribed a Higher Ordination that cannot be obtained without spending money. He prescribed Higher Ordination such that any poor candidate possessing only a bowl and robes could obtain it without spending a single cent.

Today, the practice regarding Higher Ordination is the exact opposite. Since Higher Ordination has become something to be obtained by spending money, it has become a custom in the country to send letters to relatives and lay supporters to obtain funds for the act of Higher Ordination, just as laypeople send wedding invitations to relatives and friends. During the seasons of Higher Ordination, some people receive not just one or two, but many such letters. This is a burden to the country and a cause for dissatisfaction among many. Furthermore, when the act of Higher Ordination is performed only by a specific group within each Nikāya, it allows others to charge that the entire Nikāya lacks valid Higher Ordination based on some error, thus intensifying sectarian divisions. Therefore, may the Great Community of Bhikkhus consider the suitability or unsuitability of the act of Higher Ordination being restricted to specific groups!

Since the facts to be known regarding Ecclesiastical Acts are not mentioned in a single chapter of the Vinaya Pitaka, it is not easy to learn everything necessary by reading the Vinaya texts. For bhikkhus who do not frequently use the Vinaya texts, it is difficult even to find the places where those Ecclesiastical Acts are mentioned. Therefore, for the benefit of bhikkhus who have little experience in Ecclesiastical Acts and little familiarity with the Vinaya texts, so that they may easily know how to perform those acts and perform them correctly, this volume titled "Ecclesiastical Acts" was compiled on the occasion of the Sambuddha Jayanti.

In this work, the procedure for Going Forth and the act of Higher Ordination, numerous Ecclesiastical Acts performed for the purification of the virtue of ordained bhikkhus, many acts such as the Act of Banishment performed as punishment for erring bhikkhus, the Act of Turning Over the Bowl performed as punishment for laypeople, many Ecclesiastical Acts performed for the convenience of the Community and individuals, and the difficult act of establishing a boundary have been presented in an easily understandable manner. With the help of this book, even a group of bhikkhus unskilled in the Vinaya will be able to perform an act of Higher Ordination correctly. All Ecclesiastical Acts currently performed among bhikkhus and those that bhikkhus may have to perform on various occasions are included here without omission.

The recitation of the formal act (kammavākya) must be done with great care. If an error occurs during the recitation, the act is not valid. Therefore, those who respect the Vinaya recite the formal act while looking at the text. To enable even elderly Maha Theras to read them easily, the formal acts have been printed in large type.

Printing a book of this nature, which uses several types of fonts, is a time-consuming and difficult task. However, we mention with joy that through the efficiency of Mr. W.D.J. Ratnayake Appuhamy, the manager of the Anula Press in Maradana, the work on this book was completed in a very short time.

May merit accrue to our pupil, Hengoda Kalyanadhamma, who performed with great care all the tasks such as proofreading that an author must do during the printing of a book.

May the Buddha's Dispensation shine forth!

Yours, desiring the stability of the Dispensation,

Venerable Rerukane Chandavimala Thera.
