Act of Reconciliation (Patisāraṇīya Kamma)

The Tathāgata prescribed the Act of Reconciliation on account of the Bhikkhu Sudhamma, who had reviled the householder Citta—a faithful devotee who served the Sangha—and subsequently abandoned his residence. This formal act is to be carried out against bhikkhus who insult, offend, or cause dissatisfaction among lay devotees who are faithful, settled in the Dispensation, and devoted to the Sasana. Bring the offending bhikkhu into the midst of the Community, charge him, make him remember the offense, and having made him acknowledge the transgression, perform the formal act.

The Formal Statement (Kammavācā)

Suṇātu me bhante! Saṅgho, ayaṃ (Sudhammo) bhikkhu (Cittaṃ gahapatiṃ) saddhaṃ pasannaṃ dāyakaṃ kārakaṃ saṅghupaṭṭhākaṃ hīnena khuṃsesi. Hīnena vambhesi. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ. Saṅgho (Sudhammassa) bhikkhuno paṭisāraṇīya kammaṃ kareyya, (Citto te gahapati) khamāpetabboti. Esā ñatti.

A bhikkhu against whom the Act of Reconciliation has been performed must observe the eighteen duties (vatta) prescribed for those undergoing the Act of Censure (Tajjaniya Kamma) until the act is revoked by the Community. The Community revokes the act only after the bhikkhu has asked for forgiveness from the person he offended and has restored their confidence. After the Act of Reconciliation was performed against Bhikkhu Sudhamma for offending the householder Citta, he went to Macchikāsaṇḍa to ask for forgiveness. However, being overcome with remorse, he was unable to speak to the householder Citta and returned to Sāvatthī.

When the bhikkhus inquired whether the householder Citta had forgiven him, Bhikkhu Sudhamma stated he was unable to ask. When this was reported to the Tathāgata, He allowed the Sangha to appoint a companion messenger (anudūta) to assist the bhikkhu in asking for forgiveness. In granting a companion messenger, one must first request a bhikkhu to perform the duty, obtain his consent, and then formally appoint him.

Formal Act for Granting a Companion Messenger

Suṇātu me bhante! Saṅgho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (Tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ (Sudhammassa) bhikkhuno anudūtaṃ dadeyya (Cittaṃ gahapatiṃ) khamāpetuṃ, esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! Saṅgho. Saṅgho (Tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ (Sudhammassa) bhikkhuno anudūtaṃ deti (Cittaṃ gahapatiṃ) khamāpetuṃ. Yassāyasmato khamati (Tissassa) bhikkhuno (Sudhammassa) bhikkhuno anudūtassa dānaṃ (Cittaṃ gahapatiṃ) khamāpetuṃ. So tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

Dinno saṅghena (Tisso) bhikkhu (Sudhammassa) bhikkhuno anudūto (Cittaṃ gahapatiṃ) khamāpetuṃ. Khamati saṅghassa. Tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.

The bhikkhu against whom the Act of Reconciliation was performed must go to the offended householder with the companion messenger appointed by the Community and beg for forgiveness. If the layperson does not forgive and regain confidence, the companion messenger should say: "Householder, please forgive this bhikkhu; this bhikkhu desires your goodwill."

If the householder still does not forgive, he should say: "Householder, I ask you to forgive this bhikkhu." If he still does not forgive, he should say: "Householder, forgive this bhikkhu at the request of the Community." If he still does not forgive, then in the sight and hearing of the householder, the offending bhikkhu should be made to arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, sit in the squatting position, join his hands in reverence, and confess his offense.

Revocation of the Act of Reconciliation

When the bhikkhu against whom the Act of Reconciliation was performed has obtained forgiveness from the lay devotee he insulted, has restored the devotee's confidence, and has satisfied the minds of the bhikkhus, he should approach the Community. He must arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, pay homage at the feet of the senior bhikkhus, sit in the squatting position, join his hands in reverence, and request the revocation of the Act of Reconciliation using the following words:
