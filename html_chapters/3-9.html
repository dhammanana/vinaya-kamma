Taking Dependence

A bhikkhu who has not been released from dependence due to some reason such as the passing away of his preceptor, if the preceptor's dependence is lost, that bhikkhu should live by taking dependence from a venerable, learned elder who is diligent in attendance. The bhikkhu taking dependence should approach the dependence-preceptor, arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, squat, and having paid homage, should take dependence by repeating the phrase “Ācāriyo me bhante hohi. Āyasmato nissāya vacchāmi” three times. The dependence-preceptor should accept it by saying “Pāsādikena sampādetha”. The words Sāhu, Lahu, Opāyikaṃ, Patirūpaṃ are also suitable for this purpose.

If a bhikkhu who has observed many rains retreats but lacks sufficient learning to be released from dependence cannot find an elder from whom to take dependence, he should take dependence from a younger, venerable, learned bhikkhu who is diligent in attendance. He should pay homage even to a teacher who is younger than himself. However, the word “Bhante” should not be used when addressing a junior. When an elder bhikkhu takes dependence from a junior bhikkhu, he should, in the presence of the dependence-preceptor, arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, squat, and having paid homage, say “Ācāriyo me āvuso hohi. Āyasmato nissāya vacchāmi” three times. When asking permission to enter a village, he should also squat, pay homage, and say “Gāmappavesanaṃ āpucchāmi ācariya”. The same procedure should be followed when asking questions.

Between the preceptor and the dependence-preceptor, the preceptor is primary. Dependence should be taken when one is separated from the preceptor. If, while the preceptor resides in another locality and one is living having taken dependence from another elder, the preceptor returns there, the dependence taken by the pupil ceases the moment he sees his preceptor. (It becomes null and void.) If the preceptor departs again, since the previously taken dependence has ceased, dependence must be taken again. When one receives Higher Ordination, the preceptor one has taken remains valid at all times. There is no need to take another. Dependence, however, must be taken again and again whenever it ceases.
