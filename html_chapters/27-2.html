Authorization of a Learner (Sekha).

In Savatthi, a husband and wife in a certain house were very devoted to the bhikkhus. Gradually, they fell into poverty, yet they did not reduce their giving. On certain days, they would give everything prepared in their house for the forenoon meal to the bhikkhus and remain hungry themselves. People who knew this criticized the bhikkhus, saying, "These bhikkhus accept things from others without consideration." The bhikkhus heard this and informed the Blessed One. Then the Blessed One authorized the granting of the 'Authorization of a Learner' (Sekha-sammuti) to families who are increasing in faith but decreasing in wealth.

The Procedural Text for the Authorization of a Learner.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho. (it‍thannāmaṃ) kulaṃ saddhāya vaḍḍhati. Bhogena hāyati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (itthannāmassa) kulassa sekhasammutiṃ dadey‍ya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, (it‍thannāmaṃ) kulaṃ saddhāya vaḍḍhati. Bhogena hāyati. Saṅgho (itthannāmassa) kulassa sekhasammutiṃ deti. Yassāyasmato khamati (itthannāmassa) kulassa sekhasammutiyā dānaṃ, so tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati. So bhāsey‍ya.

Dinnā saṅghena (itthannāmassa) kulassa sekhasammuti. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.

If a bhikkhu who is not ill, without an invitation, goes to a family that has received the Authorization of a Learner and, having received food with his own hand, eats it, it is an offense to be confessed (Pāṭidesanīya). There is no offense in receiving and eating something that they bring outside the house.
