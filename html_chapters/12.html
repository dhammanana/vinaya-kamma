Absolute Probation (suddhantaparivāsaya)

A bhikkhu who has committed many offenses but knows neither the number of offenses nor the number of days they were concealed must fulfill Absolute Probation. Considering the duration he lived without announcing his offenses, he must serve probation for that period, then undertake the six-day penance (Mānatta), and finally undergo the Act of Rehabilitation (Abbhāna).

If he has never made an announcement of offenses since his higher ordination, and offenses have occurred, he must fulfill the duty for the entire duration since his higher ordination.

Formula for Requesting Absolute Probation.

Ahaṃ bhante! sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpattiyo āpajjiṃ. Āpattipariyantaṃ na jānāmi. Rattipariyantaṃ na jānāmi. Āpattipariyantaṃ nassarāmi. Ratti pariyantaṃ nassarāmi. Āpattipariyante vematiko. Rattipariyante vematiko. So’haṃ bhante! saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ āpattīnaṃ suddhantaparivāsaṃ yācāmi.

Recite three times.

The Formal Act Statement for Granting Probation.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (tisso) bhikkhu sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpattiyo āpajji. Āpattipariyantaṃ na jānāti, ratti pariyantaṃ na jānāti. Āpatti pariyantaṃ nassarati. Rattipariyantaṃ nassarati, āpattipariyante vematiko, rattipariyante vematiko. So saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ āpattīnaṃ suddhantaparivāsaṃ yācati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (tissassa) bhikkhuno tāsaṃ āpattīnaṃ suddhantaparivāsaṃ dadey‍ya. Esā ñatti.

Formula for Undertaking Probation.

Parivāsaṃ samādiyāmi. Vattaṃ samādiyāmi.

Undertake by reciting three times.

Formula for Announcement.

Ahaṃ bhante! sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpattiyo āpajjiṃ. Āpattipariyantaṃ na jānāmi. Rattipariyantaṃ na jānāmi. Āpattipariyantaṃ nassarāmi. Rattipariyantaṃ nassarāmi. Āpatti pariyante vematiko. Rattipariyante vematiko. So’haṃ bhante! saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ āpattīnaṃ suddhantaparivāsaṃ yāciṃ. Tassa me saṅgho tāsaṃ āpattīnaṃ suddhantaparivāsaṃ adāsi. So’haṃ parivasāmi vedayāmahaṃ bhante vedayatīti maṃ saṅgho dhāretu.

Announce this three times. When announcing to one, two, or three bhikkhus, use the appropriate grammatical variations. When suspending (Nikkhepa), say “Parivāsaṃ nikkhipāmi. Vattaṃ nikkhipāmi” three times. The suspension is effective even with one of these phrases. The Commentary states: "Even with one word, the probation is suspended here; but with two, it is well-suspended" (Ekapadenā’pi vet‍tha nikkhitto hoti parivāso. Dvīhi pana sunikkhitto yeva). Having taken probation in this manner, go to a secluded place, reflect on your past, and fulfill the duty for a sufficient number of days to remove all doubt before taking the penance (Mānatta). Since one with many offenses may also have unconcealed offenses, when taking the penance, combine the concealed offenses for which probation was served with any unconcealed offenses, and take the penance for all of them. If the probation was in a state of suspension, you must first re-undertake and announce the probation within the boundary before requesting the penance from the Community.

Ahaṃ bhante! sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpattiyo āpajjiṃ. Appaṭicchannāyo. So’haṃ bhante! saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ paṭicchannānañca appaṭicchannānañca chārattaṃ mānattaṃ yācāmi.

After receiving the penance, while still within the boundary, arrange the upper robe over one shoulder, sit in the squatting posture, pay homage to the Community, and undertake the penance by reciting “Mānattaṃ samādiyāmi. Vattaṃ samādiyāmi” three times, then announce it to the Community.

Formula for Announcing the Penance.

Ahaṃ bhante! sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpattiyo āpajjiṃ. Āpattipariyantaṃ na jānāmi. Rattipariyantaṃ na jānāmi. Āpattipariyantaṃ nassarāmi. Rattipariyantaṃ nassarāmi. Āpattipariyante vematiko. Ratti pariyante vematiko. So’haṃ bhante! saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ āpattīnaṃ suddhantaparivāsaṃ yāciṃ. Tassa me saṅgho tāsaṃ āpattīnaṃ suddhantaparivāsaṃ adāsi. So’haṃ bhante! parivuttha parivāso. Ahaṃ bhante sambahulā saṅghādisesā āpattiyo āpajjiṃ appaṭicchannāyo. So’haṃ bhante! saṅghaṃ tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ paṭicchannānañca appaṭicchannānañca chārattaṃ mānattaṃ yāciṃ. Tassa me saṅgho tāsaṃ sambahulānaṃ āpattīnaṃ paṭicchannānañca appaṭicchannānañca chārattaṃ mānattaṃ adāsi. So’haṃ mānattaṃ carāmi. Vedayāma’haṃ bhante vedayatīti maṃ saṅgho dhāretu.

Announce this three times. Thereafter, paying homage to the Community, say “Mānattaṃ nikkhipāmi. Vattaṃ nikkhipāmi” three times to suspend the penance. Then, go and reside in a monastery where there are not less than four bhikkhus, fulfilling the penance for six days before undergoing the Act of Rehabilitation (Abbhāna). To undergo rehabilitation, assemble a group of not less than twenty bhikkhus within the boundary. If the penance was in a state of suspension, re-undertake it within the boundary, announce it to the Community, and then request rehabilitation.

There are numerous methods in the Vinaya for obtaining purification from grave offenses (Garukāpatti). Three methods are shown here, which are currently in use among bhikkhus. Through these three methods, purification from all grave offenses can be achieved. The Formal Act Statements provided here use the name ‘Tissa’. In performing a Vinaya Act for a bhikkhu, his actual name should be substituted wherever ‘Tissa’ appears in the Formal Act Statement. If there is a difficulty in doing so, the act-proclaimer may temporarily designate the name of the candidate as ‘Tissa’ and proceed with the statement as written. To designate the name, the act-proclaimer should state:

"Venerable Sirs, for the ease of proclaiming the Formal Act Statement, I designate the name ‘Tissa’ for the bhikkhu [Name], setting aside his former name. May the Community be aware of this. Let the candidate also be aware."

In this manner, the name must be designated. Once the name is designated, the Formal Act Statement must be proclaimed exactly as it appears in the book.
