Confession of Offences

‘Confession of Offences’ means the declaration for the purpose of purification from offences. Not appearing as a bhikkhu, abandoning bhikkhuhood and becoming a novice or a layperson, is the confession of a pārājikāpatti. Completing the vatta and mānatta and performing the abbhāna procedure is the confession of a saṅghādisesāpatti. By confessing to one bhikkhu, one can be purified from offences such as thullaccaya and pācittiya. Here, ‘Confession of Offences’ refers to the confession of offences such as thullaccaya. Because the training rules for bhikkhus are very numerous, and because there are also rules that can be transgressed unintentionally, bhikkhus often incur offences. Sometimes they incur offences even without knowing it.

One can easily incur the two grave offences, sukkhavissaṭṭhi and kāyasaṃsagga. When performing Monastic Procedures for purification from a grave offence, if the incurred offence has not been declared to a bhikkhu, one must undergo parivāsa for the number of days that have passed. A bhikkhu who declared the offence to another bhikkhu on the very day it was incurred can be purified by completing the mānatta for six days and performing the abbhāna procedure. It is good to declare grave offences on all occasions of confessing offences, both to avoid having to undergo vatta for grave offences and to reduce the number of days for completing the vatta. When doing so, if one definitively knows the grave offence one has incurred, it is good to declare it by naming that specific offence. By one who does not definitively know how many grave offences one has incurred, with the intention of ‘I declare all the offences I have incurred’—

one should first declare the offences by saying either “Sabbāpattiyo āvikaromi” or “Sabbāpattiyo ārocayāmi”, and then proceed with the confession of other offences.
