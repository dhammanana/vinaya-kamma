Formulations Used for Confessing Offenses

First, the junior bhikkhu should confess his offenses.

Junior Bhikkhu

-

Ahaṃ bhante! Sabbāpattiyo āvikaromi.

(This is the declaration of grave offenses.)

Senior Bhikkhu

-

Sādhu. Sādhu!

Junior Bhikkhu

-

Ahaṃ bhante! Sambahulā āpattiyo āpajjiṃ. Tā tumhamūle paṭidesemi.

Senior Bhikkhu

-

Passasi āvuso! Tā āpattiyo?

Junior Bhikkhu

-

Āma bhante! Passāmi.

Senior Bhikkhu

-

Āyatiṃ āvuso! Saṃvareyyāsi.

Junior Bhikkhu

-

Sādhu suṭṭhu bhante! Saṃvarissāmi

(Confession of offenses by the Senior Bhikkhu)

Senior Bhikkhu

-

Ahaṃ āvuso! Sabbāpattiyo āvikaromi.

Junior Bhikkhu

-

Sādhu bhante! Sādhu.

Senior Bhikkhu

-

Ahaṃ āvuso! Sambahulā āpattiyo āpajjiṃ. Tā tuyhamūle paṭidesemi.

Junior Bhikkhu

-

Passatha bhante! Tā āpattiyo?

Senior Bhikkhu

-

Āma āvuso! Passāmi.

Junior Bhikkhu

-

Āyatiṃ bhante! Saṃvareyyātha.

Senior Bhikkhu

-

Sādhu suṭṭhu ahaṃ āvuso! Saṃvarissāmi.

Junior Bhikkhu

-

Sādhu bhante! Sādhu.

When offenses have been confessed to each other in this manner, a dukkata offense remains for the bhikkhu who confessed first, due to confessing a sabhāgāpatti (an offense shared in common). If there are many sabhāgāpatti, a dukkata offense may arise for each such offense. Therefore, one should confess a second time in this manner.

Junior Bhikkhu

-

Ahaṃ bhante! Sambahulā āpattiyo āpajjiṃ. Tā tumhamūle paṭidesemi.

Senior Bhikkhu

-

Passasi āvuso! Tā āpattiyo?

Junior Bhikkhu

-

Āma bhante! Passāmi.

Senior Bhikkhu

-

Āyatiṃ āvuso! Saṃvareyyāsi.

Junior Bhikkhu

-

Sādhu suṭṭhu bhante! Saṃvarissāmi.

Senior Bhikkhu

-

Sādhu Sādhu.

Since the declaration of grave offenses was made during the first confession, there is no benefit in repeating it during the second confession.
