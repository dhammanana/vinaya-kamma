Relinquishing Nissaggiya.

If there are bowls, robes, etc., that have become nissaggiya due to not being determined (adhiṭṭhāna) or not being made use of (vikappa), purity is not attained even if the offenses are declared without relinquishing them. Therefore, in order to purify oneself from those offenses, one must relinquish the nissaggiya object and declare the offense. A bhikkhu who uses nissaggiya robes, etc., without relinquishing them incurs a dukkata offense each time he uses them. It should be understood that relinquishing is not a gift or an offering, but a Monastic Procedure. A nissaggiya object can be relinquished to the Community of monks, to a group (gaṇa), or to an individual. Since relinquishing to an individual is easier, only the method of relinquishing to an individual is presented here.

Take a robe that has become nissaggiya by exceeding ten days without being determined or made use of to one bhikkhu, sit squatting, take the robe in hand, and say the statement: “Idaṃ me bhante! Cīvaraṃ dasāhātikkantaṃ nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ āyasmato nissajāmi”, then give the robe and declare the offense. After the offense is declared, the bhikkhu who received it should return it, saying: “Imaṃ cīvaraṃ āyasmato dammi”. If there are many robes, one should say: “Imāni me bhante cīvarāni dasāhātikkantāni. Nissaggiyāni imānāhaṃ āyasmato nissajāmi”. When returning them, one should say: “Imāni cīvarāni āyasmato dammi”.

When relinquishing robes that are outside one's reach, one should say: “Etaṃ me āvuso! Cīvaraṃ dasāhātikkantaṃ nissaggiyaṃ, etāhaṃ āyasmato nissajāmi”. When returning it, one should say: “Etaṃ cīvaraṃ āyasmato dammi”. If there is more than one robe, one should say: “Etāni me bhante! Cīvarāni dasāhātikkantāni nissaggiyāni” and “Etānāhaṃ āyasmato nissajāmi”, and “Etāni cīvarāni āyasmato dammi”.

A robe that has become nissaggiya by being separated overnight (rattivitvuttha) should be relinquished by saying: “Idamme bhante! Cīvaraṃ ratti vippavutthaṃ aññatra bhikkhu sammutiyā nissaggiyaṃ. Imāhaṃ āyasmato nissajāmi”.

An extra bowl kept for more than ten days should be relinquished by saying: “Ayamme patto dasāhātikkanto nissaggiyo. Imāhaṃ āyasmato nissajāmi”. The method for relinquishing other nissaggiya objects should be looked up in the Vinaya books. These can be easily found in the Kudusika.
