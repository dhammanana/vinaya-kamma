The Act of Charging with Vexation

The Act of Charging with Vexation is an act performed against bhikkhus who, when being questioned in the midst of the Community regarding an offense, remain silent and thereby vex the Community.

The text of the Act is as follows:

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (channo) bhikkhu saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjiyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṅghaṃ viheseti. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (channassa) bhikkhuno vihesakaṃ ropey‍ya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (channo) bhikkhu saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjiyamāno tuṇhībhūto saṅghaṃ viheseti. Saṅgho (channassa) bhikkhuno vihesakaṃ ropeti. Yassāyasmato khamati. (channassa) bhikkhuno vihesakassa ropanā. So tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati so bhāsey‍ya.

Ropitaṃ saṅghena (channassa) bhikkhuno vihesakaṃ. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.

After this act has been performed, a bhikkhu who vexes the Community by not speaking when questioned about an offense in the midst of the Community incurs a Pācittiya offense.

The Act of Formal Rebuking for bhikkhus holding evil views.

In the time of the Buddha, a bhikkhu named Ariṭṭha held the view: "I understand the Dhamma taught by the Blessed One in such a way that those things called obstructive states by the Blessed One are not actually able to obstruct one who indulges in them." He argued that laypeople who enjoy the five cords of sensual pleasure become Stream-enterers, Once-returners, and Non-returners; and that bhikkhus also attain Arahantship while seeing pleasant forms, hearing sounds, smelling scents, tasting flavors, and experiencing the touch of soft garments. Therefore, he claimed there is no reason why the single act of touching a woman should be obstructive. On account of that matter, the Buddha prescribed the Act of Formal Rebuking for bhikkhus who hold such views and firmly cling to them without abandoning them.

It is performed thus: Bhikkhus who see or hear of a bhikkhu holding such an evil view must admonish him up to three times to abandon that view. If he does not abandon the view upon being admonished, he incurs a Dukkaṭa offense. Bhikkhus who see or hear of it but do not admonish him also incur a Dukkaṭa offense. If he does not abandon the view after being admonished, bring that bhikkhu into the midst of the Community and admonish him up to three times. If he does not comply, he incurs another Dukkaṭa offense. If he still does not abandon it, perform the Act of Formal Rebuking against that bhikkhu.

The text of the Act.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, (tissassa) bhikkhuno evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ, tathā haṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi, yathā ye me antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā, te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyāti. So taṃ diṭṭhiṃ na paṭinissajjati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ samanubhāsey‍ya tassā diṭṭhiyā paṭinissaggāya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, (tissassa) bhikkhuno evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ, “Tathāhaṃ bhagavatā dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi yathā ye me antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyāti.” so taṃ diṭṭhiṃ na paṭinissajjati. Saṅgho (tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ samanubhāsati tassā diṭṭhiyā paṭinissaggāya. Yassāyasmato khamati (tissassa) bhikkhuno samanubhāsanā tassā diṭṭhiyā paṭinissaggāya. So tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati, so bhāsey‍ya.

Dutiyampi etamat‍thaṃ vadāmi. -pe- tatiyampi etamat‍thaṃ vdāmi.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, (tissassa) bhikkhuno evarūpaṃ pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ uppannaṃ, tathāhaṃ bhagavato dhammaṃ desitaṃ ājānāmi. Yathā ye me antarāyikā dhammā vuttā bhagavatā te paṭisevato nālaṃ antarāyāyāti. So taṃ diṭṭhiṃ na paṭinissajjati. Saṅgho (tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ samanubhāsati tassā diṭṭhiyā paṭinissaggāya. Yassāyasmato khamati (tissassa) bhikkhuno samanubhāsanā tassā diṭṭhiyā paṭinissaggāya. So tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati, so bhāsey‍ya.

Samanubhaṭṭho saṅghena (tisso) bhikkhu tassā diṭṭhiyā paṭinissaggāya. Khamati saṅghassa tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.

Immediately upon the conclusion of this act, the bhikkhu holding the view incurs a Pācittiya offense.

The Act of Expulsion for Sāmaṇeras holding wrong views.

If a Sāmaṇera speaks in dispraise of the Buddha, the Dhamma, or the Sangha, or says that what is unallowable is allowable, or holds wrong views, admonish him up to three times to abandon that fault. If the Sāmaṇera does not abandon that view, assemble the Community and tell him in the midst of the Community to abandon that view. If the Sāmaṇera abandons the view, it is well. If he does not abandon it, perform the Act of Expulsion against him. Among the four types of legal acts, this is an Act of Announcement (Apolokana-kamma). When performing it, a competent bhikkhu should announce the expulsion of the Sāmaṇera with the following text:

The text of the Act.

Saṅghaṃ bhante! pucchāmi, ayaṃ (kaṇṭhako) sāmaṇero buddhassa dhammassa saṅghassa avaṇṇavādi micchādiṭṭhiko, yaṃ aññe sāmaṇerā labhanti bhikkhūhi saddhiṃ dvirattatirattaṃ sahasey‍ya, tassā alābhāya nissāraṇā ruccati saṅghassa.

Dutiyampi saṅghaṃ bhante pucchāmi. -pe- nissāraṇā ruccati saṅghassa.

Tatiyampi saṅghaṃ bhante pucchāmi. -pe- nissāraṇā ruccati saṅghassa.

Having thus informed the Community, say to the Sāmaṇera: "Go, unprincipled Sāmaṇera, be destroyed!" A Sāmaṇera who has been thus expelled must be shunned by all bhikkhus. A bhikkhu who supports such an expelled Sāmaṇera, or accepts services from him, or shares Dhamma or material things with him, or sleeps in the same lodging with him, incurs a Pācittiya offense.
