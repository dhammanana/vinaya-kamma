Formal Act Statement

The Formal Act Statement is as follows:—

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho. Idaṃ saṅghassa kaṭhinadussaṃ uppannaṃ. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ. Saṅgho imaṃ kaṭhinadussaṃ (tissassa) bhikkhuno dadey‍ya kaṭhinaṃ at‍tharituṃ. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho. Idaṃ saṅghassa kaṭhinadussaṃ uppannaṃ. Saṅgho imaṃ kaṭhinadussaṃ (tissassa) bhikkhuno deti kaṭhinaṃ at‍tharituṃ. Yassāyasmato khamati imassa kaṭhinadussassa (tissassa) bhikkhuno dānaṃ kaṭhinaṃ at‍tharituṃ. So tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati so bhāsey‍ya.

Dinnaṃ idaṃ saṅghena kaṭhinadussaṃ (tissassa) bhikkhuno kaṭhinaṃ at‍tharituṃ khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.

If the Kathina cloth given to a bhikkhu by the Community in this manner is a cloth or robe that has been cut, sewn, and dyed by the donors themselves and prepared so as to be suitable for determination, then, while seated within the boundary where the Rains-residence was observed, mark it with the bindu. If it is a double-layered robe (sanghati), relinquish the old sanghati and determine the new one by reciting three times: “Imaṃ saṅghāṭiṃ adhiṭṭhāmi”. If it is an upper robe (uttarasanga), relinquish the old uttarasanga and determine the new one by reciting three times: “Imaṃ uttarāsaṅgaṃ adhiṭṭhāmi”. If it is an inner robe (antaravasaka), relinquish the old antaravasaka and determine the new one by reciting three times: “Imaṃ antaravāsakaṃ adhiṭṭhāmi”.

Then, taking that robe in hand, if it is the sanghati, recite three times: “Imāya saṅghāṭiyā kaṭhinaṃ attharāmi”. If it is the uttarasanga, recite: “Iminā uttarāsaṅghena kaṭhinaṃ attharāmi”. If it is the antaravasaka, recite: “Iminā antaravāsakena kaṭhinaṃ attharāmi”. Reciting thus constitutes the Monastic Procedure for Spreading the Kathina. When this is performed, that bhikkhu receives the five benefits, such as wandering without having asked for permission (anamantacara), as decreed by the Buddha. Subsequently, that bhikkhu should have the spreading of the Kathina cloth approved.

Perform it in this way:—

The bhikkhu should arrange his robe over one shoulder, place his hands in the gesture of reverence (anjali), and if he is the senior-most monk (Sanghatthera), he should say three times to the Community: “Atthataṃ āvuso saṅghassa kaṭhinaṃ dhammiko kaṭhinatthāro anumodatha”. If there are elders present, he should say: “Atthataṃ bhante saṅghassa kaṭhinaṃ dhammiko kaṭhinatthāro anumodatha”. The bhikkhus who are to approve should arrange their robes over one shoulder, sit in the squatting posture (ukkutika), place their hands in the gesture of reverence, and approve the spreading of the Kathina by reciting: “Atthataṃ bhante saṅghassa kaṭhinaṃ dhammiko kaṭhinatthāro anumodāmi”. Thereafter, the bhikkhus who have approved also gain the five benefits. If the Kathina cloth is an unfinished cloth—

“Pubbakaraṇaṃ sattahi dhammehi saṅgahitaṃ, dhovanena vicāraṇena chedanena bandhanena sibbanena rajanena kappakaraṇena”

The seven preliminary duties—namely: washing, marking for cutting, cutting, tacking, sewing, dyeing, and marking with the bindu—must be completed on that very day, and the spreading of the Kathina must be performed on that same day.

“Kaṭhinaṃ kena dinnaṃ vaṭṭati? Yena kenaci devena vā manussena vā pañcannaṃ vā sahadhammikānaṃ aññatarena dinnaṃ vaṭṭati” According to the Commentary, a cloth given by any human, or a deity, or any of the five classes of co-religionists (bhikkhu, bhikkhuni, samanera, samaneri, or sikkhamana) is suitable for the Kathina.

If a lay devotee who does not know how to give the Kathina asks how it should be given, they should be told that a cloth sufficient for one of the three robes should be given at sunrise with the words, "We give this Kathina cloth," and that needles, thread, and dye for its preparation, as well as almsfood for the bhikkhus participating in it, should be provided. This is stated in the Vinaya Commentary. Regarding the giving of the Kathina, there is not even a single word mentioned in the Vinaya Pali stating that specifically a cloth must be given or a finished robe must be given. The Commentary's statement that 'a cloth sufficient for one of the three robes should be given' is not because a finished robe should not be given, but according to the custom prevalent at that time. In ancient times, the sewing of robes was done by the bhikkhus. Donors offered only the cloth for the robes.

“Saṅghena pana kassa dātabbaṃ? Yo jiṇṇa cīvaro hoti. Sace bahu chinnacīvarā. Buḍḍhassa dātabbaṃ. Buḍḍhesupi yo mahāpariso tadaheva cīvaraṃ katvā attharituṃ sakkoti. Tassa dātabbaṃ. Sace buḍḍho na sakkoti navakataro sakkoti. Tassa dātabbaṃ. ‘Api ca saṅghena mahātherassa saṅgahaṃ kātuṃ vaṭṭati. Tasmā tumhe bhante gaṇhatha. Mayaṃ katvā dassāmi’ti vattabbaṃ”

As stated in the Commentary, the cloth offered for the Kathina by a donor should be given by the Community to that bhikkhu among the group whose robes are worn out. If there are no such bhikkhus, or if many have worn-out robes, the cloth should be given to the senior-most (vuddha). Among the seniors, it should be given to one who has a large following capable of completing the cutting, sewing, and dyeing of the robe on that very day to spread the Kathina. If the senior is not capable but a junior is capable, the Kathina cloth should be given to the junior. It is proper for the Community to show regard to the Chief Elder (Mahathera). Therefore, the Community should say to the Great Elder, "Venerable Sir, please accept it; we shall prepare it for you."
