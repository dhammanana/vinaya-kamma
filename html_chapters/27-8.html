The Monastic Procedure for Bestowing Robes to the Deceased.

If a bhikkhu passes away, all that belongs to that bhikkhu becomes the property of the Community. The notion that “the belongings of deceased bhikkhus belong to their disciples” is a Vinaya-contrary rule established in modern times according to the system of property ownership among laypeople.

“Bhikkhussa bhikkhave! kālakate saṅgho sāmi pattacīvare. Api ca gilānupaṭṭhākā bahukārā. Anujānāmi bhikkhave! saṅghena ticīvaraṃ ca pattaṃ ca gilānupaṭṭhākānaṃ dātuṃ. Yaṃ tattha lahubhaṇḍaṃ lahuparikkhāraṃ taṃ sammukhībhūtena saṅghena bhājetuṃ, yaṃ tattha garubhaṇḍaṃ garuparikkhāraṃ taṃ āgatānāgatassa cātuddisassa saṅghassa avissajjikaṃ avebhaṅgikaṃ” This is the Vinaya rule concerning the requisites of deceased bhikkhus, as stated in the Cīvarakkhandhaka of the Mahāvagga Pāḷi.

The attendant bhikkhu should take the bowl and robes of the bhikkhu who has passed away to the Community, and inform the Community: “Itthannāmo Bhante! Bhikkhu kālakato, idaṃ tassa ticīvaraṃ ca patto ca”. Although these belong to the Community, they should be given by the Community to the bhikkhu who attended the sick.

The Monastic Procedure Text.

Suṇātu me Bhante! Saṅgho. (Itthannāmo) bhikkhu kālakato. Idaṃ tassa ticīvaraṃ ca patto ca. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho imaṃ ticīvaraṃ ca pattaṃ ca gilānupaṭṭhākānaṃ dadeyya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me Bhante! Saṅgho. (Itthannāmo) bhikkhu kālakato, idaṃ tassa ticīvaraṃ ca patto ca, saṅgho imaṃ ticīvaraṃ ca pattaṃ ca gilānupaṭṭhākānaṃ deti. Yassāyasmato khamati imassa ticīvarassa ca pattassa ca gilānupaṭṭhākānaṃ dānaṃ, so tuṇhassu. Yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

Dinnaṃ saṅghena ticīvaraṃ ca patto ca gilānupaṭṭhākānaṃ. Khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.
