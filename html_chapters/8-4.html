Individual Pavāraṇā for a Small Group

The Pavāraṇā performed when there are three or two bhikkhus is Individual Pavāraṇā. If there are three bhikkhus assembled within the sīmā, a competent bhikkhu should—

“Suṇantu me āyasmantā ajja pavāraṇā paṇṇarasi yadāyasmantānaṃ pattakallaṃ mayaṃ aññamaññaṃ pavāreyyāma.”

Thus, the Motion should be laid. Thereupon, the senior bhikkhu, squatting and clasping his hands in añjali, should—

Ahaṃ āvuso āyasmante pavāremi. Diṭṭhena vā sutena vā parisaṅkāya vā vadantu maṃ āyasmantā anukampaṃ upādāya passanto paṭikarissāmi. Dutiyam-pi -pe- tatiyam-pi -pe- paṭikarissāmi.

Thus, you should perform Pavāraṇā towards the other two bhikkhus. The junior bhikkhus should perform Pavāraṇā by saying, “Ahaṃ bhante,” and so forth.

These two words, “Āyasmanto—Āyasmantā,” should be especially kept in mind. Āyasmanto is plural. Āyasmantā is dual. In the Buddha's Dispensation, the only word used in the dual sense is “Āyasmantā.”

If there are only two bhikkhus, without laying a Motion, the senior bhikkhu, having draped his outer robe over one shoulder, squatting, and clasping his hands in añjali, should address the other bhikkhu thus: “Ahaṃ āvuso āyasmantaṃ pavāremi. Diṭṭhena vā sutena vā parisaṅkāya vā vadatu maṃ āyasmā anukampaṃ upādāya. Passanto paṭikarissāmi. Dutiyam-pi -pe- tatiyam-pi -pe- paṭikarissāmi.” The junior bhikkhu should perform Pavāraṇā towards the senior bhikkhu using the same sentence, substituting ‘bhante’.
