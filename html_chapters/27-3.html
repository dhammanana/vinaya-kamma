Authorization for a Robe-Receiver.

In the time of the Buddha, people would bring robes to the monastery to offer to the Community. Unable to find anyone to receive them, the people would take the robes they had brought back with them. As a result, the Community received fewer robes. The bhikkhus reported this to the Tathāgata. Thereupon, the Tathāgata permitted the authorization of a bhikkhu as a robe-receiver, one who would not go astray, who could give a good word of appreciation, and who was capable of inspiring people.

Formal Statement for the Authorization of a Robe-Receiver.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ cīvarapaṭiggāhakaṃ sammanney‍ya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, saṅgho (tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ cīvarapaṭiggāhakaṃ sammannati. Yassāyasmato khamati (tissassa) bhikkhuno cīvarapaṭiggāhakassa sammuti. So tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati, so bhāsey‍ya.

Sammato saṅghena (tisso) bhikkhu cīvarapaṭiggāhako. Khamati saṅghassa. Tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.
