Shaving (Bhandukamma)

The first Vinaya procedure that bhikkhus must perform when admitting a faithful clansman—who desires to realize Nibbana—into this noble, emancipating Dispensation established by our Blessed, Worthy, and Rightly Self-Awakened One to release helpless beings wandering in Samsara and burning with the fires of suffering, is called ‘Shaving’ (Bhaṇḍukamma). It is a Vinaya procedure concerning the shaving of the hair of candidates for the Going Forth (pabbajjā).

During the time the Buddha was dwelling [in the world], a certain son of a family quarreled with his parents, fled from home, and went to a monastery. There, he received the Going Forth from a certain Elder. This was done privately, and many bhikkhus were unaware of it. The parents of the child, searching for him, arrived at that monastery and asked the bhikkhus they encountered, “Venerable Sirs, have you seen such a child? Do you know where such a child has gone?” Because those bhikkhus had not seen the child, they said, “We have not seen him,” and because they did not know, they said, “We do not know about that child.”

The parents, not being satisfied with this, searched here and there and eventually saw the child who had gone forth. Becoming angry with the bhikkhus, they reviled them, saying, “These bhikkhus say they have not seen what they have seen, and they say they do not know what they know; they are liars. These lying bhikkhus are shameless (alajjī) and immoral,” and so forth. The bhikkhus reported this matter to the Tathagata. Thereupon, the Blessed One, so that such a thing might not happen in the future, prescribed the Shaving procedure, saying:

“Anujānāmi bhikkhave! saṅghaṃ apalokatuṃ bhaṇḍukammāya” (I allow you, monks, to ask the Community for the shaving.)

This procedure should be performed when the hair of one who has hair longer than two finger-breadths is being cut by a bhikkhu or by the order of a bhikkhu. Perform it in this manner: Assemble all the bhikkhus residing within the monastery and bring the candidate for the Going Forth before the Community. The teacher or another bhikkhu shall then announce before the Community:

“Saṅghaṃ bhante! imassa dārakassa bhaṇḍukammaṃ āpucchāmi” (Venerable Sirs, I ask the Community for the shaving of this boy.) Recite this sentence once, twice, or thrice. For the Shaving, it is also proper to say “Imassa dārakassa bhaṇḍukammaṃ āpucchāmi” or “Imassa samaṇa karaṇaṃ āpucchāmi” or “Ayaṃ samaṇo hotu kāmo”.

Without assembling the bhikkhus, it is also proper to go to the places where the individual bhikkhus reside and inquire regarding the Shaving. The preceptor himself need not go to the bhikkhus; it is also proper to send another to inquire from them. If the procedure must be performed at a time when bhikkhus are so numerous that it is difficult to assemble them all or to inform them individually, go to a formal boundary (baddha-sīmā) or a water-boundary (udakukkhepa-sīmā) and perform the procedure in the presence of the bhikkhus assembled there.

The shaving of the head of one who is to go forth is a significant matter. If the person going forth is one of merit, virtue, and wisdom, who can be of special benefit to the Sasana in the future, the teachers should take special care regarding such individuals. When ordaining such a person, the best method is for the teacher himself to take the candidate to the place of water and shave the head, rather than sending the candidate away to shave and bathe alone.

The moment of going forth is a time when a clansman's mind is very clear and confident. Therefore, what is taught at that time is deeply impressed upon his mind. The occasion of shaving the head is the most opportune time to instruct the candidate in the ‘Meditation on the Fivefold Skin-parts’ (taca-pañcaka-kammaṭṭhāna). Certain clansmen with the requisite supporting conditions (upanissaya-sampatti), by reflecting on the taca-pañcaka-kammaṭṭhāna taught at this moment, directing the mind accordingly, defining mind and matter (nāma-rūpa), and seeing the three characteristics of impermanence etc. in formations, realize the Four Noble Truths and attain Arahantship through the stages of the Path and Fruit even while the hair is being shaved, destroying all defilements and realizing Nibbana. There are many mentions in our texts of individuals who became Arahants while their hair was being shaved.

Even for clansmen who do not have the supporting conditions to become Arahants at that very moment, what is taught then is of great benefit as it is deeply impressed upon the mind. To ensure the candidate benefits from this occasion, the teacher should shave the head while instructing him in the taca-pañcaka-kammaṭṭhāna. If the teacher is unable to shave the head and has another do it, the teacher should stand nearby and instruct the candidate in the taca-pañcaka-kammaṭṭhāna. Alternatively, the teacher should appoint another bhikkhu to do so. In doing this, first place the lock of hair that is cut into the hand of the candidate, tell him to look at it well and take its nature to heart, and then instruct him in the meditation.
