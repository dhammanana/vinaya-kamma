Authorization of a Meal-Assigner.

Those wishing to offer alms come to monasteries where many bhikkhus reside and invite bhikkhus to their homes in groups of five, ten, and so forth. A bhikkhu should be authorized by the Community to accept invitations made to the Community and to designate and dispatch bhikkhus for them. The authorized bhikkhu, having accepted the invitations, should record the places to be visited for alms on slips of wood, bamboo, or leaves, mix them all together, and distribute the voting-slips (salāka) in order, starting from the standing roster (ṭhitikā) or, if there is no standing roster, starting from the senior Elder. The bhikkhus who have received the voting-slips should go to the places mentioned on their respective slips. This is the method for assigning meals.

The Procedural Text for the Authorization.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ bhattuddesakaṃ sammanney‍ya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, saṅgho (tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ bhattuddesakaṃ sammannati. Yassāyasmato khamati (tissassa) bhikkhuno bhattuddesakassa sammuti so tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati, so bhāsey‍ya.

Sammato saṅghena (tisso) bhikkhu bhattuddesako, khamati saṅghassa, tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.
