The Four Monastic Procedures.

Monastic Procedures are of four types: Apalokana-kamma (Declaration Procedure), Ñatti-kamma (Motion Procedure), Ñatti-dutiya-kamma (Motion-and-one-Proclamation Procedure), and Ñatti-catuttha-kamma (Motion-and-three-Proclamations Procedure).

The procedure performed by assembling the Community within the boundary, taking the consent of those who cannot attend, and announcing the matter to the Community three times is called ‘Apalokana-kamma’ (Declaration Procedure). The procedure performed, as stated, with the approval of the Community, solely by a Motion, such as “Suṇātu me bhante saṅgho ajjupposatho paṇṇaraso yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho uposathaṃ kareyya”, is called ‘Ñatti-kamma’ (Motion Procedure). The procedure performed with the approval of the Community, with a Motion and one proclamation, is called ‘Ñatti-dutiya-kamma’ (Motion-and-one-Proclamation Procedure). The procedure performed with one Motion and three proclamations is called ‘Ñatti-catuttha-kamma’ (Motion-and-three-Proclamations Procedure).

In the procedural texts, the initial section from ‘Suṇātu me bhante saṅgho’ up to ‘esā ñatti’ is the Motion. The subsequent sections of text, such as “Suṇātu me bhante saṅgho”, are the proclamation.

Among these Monastic Procedures, what should be performed as an Apalokana-kamma must be done by declaration only, and not as a Ñatti-kamma or other such procedure. A Ñatti-kamma must be performed with a Motion. It must not be performed as an Apalokana-kamma or other such procedure. Among the Ñatti-dutiya-kamma procedures, there are those that should be performed by declaration and those that should not. The six procedures of boundary Authorizations (sīmā-sammuti), boundary revocation (sīmā-samūhanana), offering the Kathina robe (kaṭhina-dāna), revoking the Kathina (kaṭhinuddhāra), declaring a site for a hut (kuṭi vatthu desanā), and declaring a site for a dwelling (vihāra vatthu desanā) are weighty procedures (garu-kamma); therefore, they must be performed by reciting the Ñatti-dutiya-kamma text itself. They must not be performed by declaration. The remaining thirteen Authorizations, and the Authorizations for receiving a dwelling (senāsana-gāhaka-sammuti) and for offering a dead monk's robe (mataka-cīvara-dāna-sammuti), are light procedures (laghu-kamma); therefore, there is no fault in performing them by declaration. A Ñatti-kamma must absolutely not be performed as a Ñatti-catuttha-kamma. If there is an error in letters or words when reciting the procedural text, it is proper to recite it again and again for purification. Doing so is for the accomplishment of an unaccomplished procedure, and for the strengthening of an accomplished procedure. A Ñatti-catuttha-kamma must be performed with a Motion and three proclamations. It must not be performed as an Apalokana-kamma or other such procedure.
