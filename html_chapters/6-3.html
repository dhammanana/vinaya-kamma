Method for Performing the Sangha Uposatha

Making the Invitation

“Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ ahaṃ āyasmantaṃ (tissaṃ) pātimokkhuddesaṃ ajjhesey‍yaṃ.” Having obtained the authorization in this manner, make the invitation by reciting the following sentence three times:

Saṅgho bhante! āyasmantaṃ (tissaṃ) pātimokkhuddesaṃ ajjhesati. Uddisatu āyasmā (tisso) pātimokkhaṃ.

Giving the Response

Āma uddisissāmahaṃ āvuso.

Authorization of the Questioner of the Discipline

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ. Ahaṃ āyasmantaṃ (dattaṃ) vinayaṃ pucchey‍yaṃ.

Authorization of the Answerer

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ. Ahaṃ āyasmatā (tissena) vinayaṃ puṭṭho vissajjey‍yaṃ.

Interrogating the Discipline

“Sammajjanī padipo ca

Udakaṃ āsanena ca

Uposathassa etāni

Pubbakaraṇanti vuccati”

Have the four preliminary duties to be performed before the Community assembles in the Uposatha hall—namely, sweeping the Uposatha hall, lighting the lamps, setting out the water, and preparing the seats—been completed?

Answer: Since there is currently sunlight, there is no need to light the lamps. The other three duties have been completed.

“Chandapārisuddhi utukkhānaṃ

Bhikkhu gaṇanā ca ovādo

Uposathassa etāni

Pubbakiccanti vuccati”

In order to complete the preliminary obligations as mentioned, has the conveyance of the consent and purity (chanda-pārisuddhi) been announced to the Community?

Answer: Since all the bhikkhus within the boundary are assembled here, there is no conveyance of consent and purity.

Question: The seasons are three: the cold season, the hot season, and the rainy season. Of these, which is the current season?

Give the answer.

Question: In each season there are eight fortnights. In this current season, how many fortnights have passed? How many fortnights remain? Which fortnight is this current one?

Give the answer.

Question: How many bhikkhus are currently assembled within this boundary?

State the number.

Question: Has the request made by the bhikkhunīs for the sake of receiving exhortation been announced to the Community?

Answer: Since there are no bhikkhunīs at this time, there is no such request.

Question: These four are called preliminary obligations because they must be performed after the Community has assembled within the boundary but before the recitation of the Pātimokkha.

“Uposatho yāvatikā ca bhikkhu

Kammappattā sabhāgāpattiyo ca

Na vijjanti vajjanīyā ca puggalā

Tasmiṃ na honti pattakallanti vuccati”

As declared by the Commentators, the appropriate time for the Uposatha procedure must be endowed with four factors. Although the Uposatha is one by general characteristic, it is threefold by way of days: the fourteenth, the fifteenth, and the day of reconciliation (sāmaggī); threefold by way of participating individuals: the Community, a group, and an individual; and threefold by way of the manner of performance: recitation of the Suttas (suttuddesa), declaration of purity (pārisuddhi), and determination (adhiṭṭhāna). Of these, what kind of Uposatha is this being performed, in terms of the day?

Give the answer.

Question: What kind of Uposatha is it in terms of the participating individuals?

Give the answer.

Question: What kind of Uposatha is it in terms of the manner of performance?

Give the answer.

Question: When it is said to be a 'recitation of the Suttas' (suttuddesa) Uposatha, a group of no fewer than four bhikkhus must remain within one boundary without leaving the proximity (hatthapāsa). Are that many bhikkhus present here?

Answer, "Yes."

Question: Are the assembled bhikkhus free from common offenses (sabhāgāpatti)?

Give the answer.

Question: Have the twenty-one types of excluded persons, such as laypeople, been removed from the proximity of the Community?

Answer, "Yes."

Then, the Elder reciting the Pātimokkha should speak as follows:

This procedure endowed with these four factors is called 'Timely'. Pubbakaraṇa pubbakiccāni samāpetvā desitāpattikassa samaggassa bhikkhusaṅghassa anumatiyā pātimokkhaṃ uddisituṃ ārādhanaṃ karoma.

When this is said, the Community should give the Sadhu-exclamation. Then, having stated the Formal Statement (ñatti), the Pātimokkha should be recited.

A Special Note

“Pubbakaraṇa pubbakiccāni samāpetvā” —and so forth—is a passage found in the Pātimokkha, but it is not seen in the Vinaya Pali or the Commentaries. Even in the Kaṅkhāvitaraṇī Commentary, which explains the Pātimokkha, there is no mention of this passage. From this, it is to be understood that this passage was included in the Pātimokkha at a later period. The first part of it is found in various manuscripts as “Pubbakaraṇa pubbakiccāni samāpetvā” and “Samādapetvā”.

The meaning of “Pātimokkhaṃ uddisituṃ ārādhanaṃ karoma” is misunderstood by many. It is not something said by the bhikkhu who invites the recitation of the Pātimokkha, but rather by the Elder who is actually reciting the Pātimokkha. Therefore, the meaning of “Ārādhanaṃ karoma” should be taken as 'I endeavor' to recite the Pātimokkha. It should not be taken as 'we request the recitation of the Pātimokkha'. The author of the Vinayālaṅkāra-ṭīkā expresses a dislike for that passage. The author of the Vinayālaṅkāra-ṭīkā states that one should say: “Pubbakaraṇa pubbakiccāni sammā niṭṭhāpetvā pattakallaṅge samānīte saṅghassa anumatiyā pātimokkhaṃ uddissāma”. It should be noted that however this is said, it does not cause any harm to the Monastic Procedure.
