Authorization of an Alms-bowl Distributor.

There are two types of authorizations for an Alms-bowl Distributor. One is the authorization to distribute bowls that have been received by the Community; this is set forth in the Senāsanakkhandhaka of the Cūḷavaggapāḷi. The other is the authorization to cause the bhikkhus to accept a bowl forfeited to the Community by a bhikkhu who has incurred an offense by obtaining a new bowl when his current bowl has fewer than five mends, according to the training rule: “Yo pana bhikkhu ūnapañcabandhanena pattena aññaṃ navaṃ pattaṃ cetāpeyya nissaggiyaṃ pācittiyaṃ”. This is declared in the Pārājikāpāḷi. There is no difference in the formal act sentences (kammavācā) between the two.

The formal act is as follows:

Suṇātu me bhante! Saṅgho, yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (Tisso) bhikkhuṃ pattagāhāpakaṃ sammanneyya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! Saṅgho, saṅgho (Tissaṃ) bhikkhuṃ pattagāhāpakaṃ sammannati. Yassāyasmato khamati (Tissassa) bhikkhuno pattagāhāpakassa sammuti. So tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati, so bhāseyya.

Sammato saṅghena (Tisso) bhikkhu pattagāhāpako, khamati saṅghassa. Tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.

The bhikkhu who obtained a new bowl while possessing one with fewer than five mends should approach the Community, arrange his upper robe over one shoulder, pay homage at the feet of the senior bhikkhus, sit in the squatting position, and with joined palms [say]:

“Ayaṃ me bhante! patto ūna pañcabandhanena pattena cetāpito nissaggiyo. Ima’haṃ saṅghassa nissajjāmi”

Having thus forfeited the bowl to the Community, he should confess the offense. The Community must assemble for this procedure with the bowl present. To cause the bhikkhus to accept the forfeited bowl, a bhikkhu must be authorized using the aforementioned formal act. The authorized bhikkhu should take that bowl, describe its qualities, and offer it to the Elder of the Community. If that bowl is better than his own, the Elder of the Community should accept it and give his own bowl to the authorized bhikkhu. If he does not accept it out of compassion for the original owner, the Elder incurs an offense of wrong-doing (dukkaṭa). There is no offense if he does not accept it because the bowl is of poor quality. If the Elder of the Community accepts that bowl, his bowl should then be offered to the second senior monk. In this manner, having offered the bowls to all the bhikkhus, the final remaining bowl, which will be the most inferior, should be given to the bhikkhu who [wrongfully] requested the bowl.
