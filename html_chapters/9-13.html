The Formal Announcement for the Revocation of the Kathina

The formal announcement for the revocation of the Kathina is as follows:

“Suṇātu me bhante! Saṅgho. Yadi Saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho kaṭhinaṃ uddhareyya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! Saṅgho. Saṅgho kaṭhinaṃ uddharati. Yassāyasmato khamati kaṭhinassa ubbhāro. So tuṇhasssa. Yassa nakkhamati so bhāseyya. Ubbhataṃ Saṅghena kaṭhinaṃ. Khamati Saṅghassa. Tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.”

If the benefits based on the revocation of the Kathina are greater than the benefits based on the spreading of the Kathina, then the Kathina should be revoked. If the benefits based on the revocation are few, it should not be revoked. For the sake of preserving the donor's faith, the Kathina should also be revoked when the benefits are equal. Opposing a righteous revocation of the Kathina incurs an offense.
