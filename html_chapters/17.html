The Formal Act of Charging with Evasive Speech

The Formal Act of Charging with Evasive Speech is a procedure prescribed for those bhikkhus who, having committed improper actions and fallen into offences, attempt to conceal their fault by speaking of something else when questioned in the midst of the Community.

The formal text of the act is as follows:

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (channo) bhikkhu saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjiyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati. Yadi saṅghassa pattakallaṃ, saṅgho (channassa) bhikkhuno aññavādakaṃ ropey‍ya. Esā ñatti.

Suṇātu me bhante! saṅgho, ayaṃ (channo) bhikkhu saṅghamajjhe āpattiyā anuyuñjiyamāno aññenaññaṃ paṭicarati. Saṅgho (channassa) bhikkhuno aññavādakaṃ ropeti. Yassāyasmato khamati (channassa) bhikkhuno aññavādakassa ropanā. So tuṇhassa. Yassa nakkhamati, so bhāsey‍ya.

Ropitaṃ saṅghena (channassa) bhikkhuno aññavādakaṃ. Khamati saṅghassa. Tasmā tuṇhī. Evametaṃ dhārayāmi.

Prior to the performance of this formal act, speaking evasively when questioned in the midst of the Community entails an offence of wrongdoing (dukkaṭa). After the charge of evasive speech has been formally laid, speaking evasively entails an offence to be confessed (pācittiya).
