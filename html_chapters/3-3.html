The Four Resources

Venerable one, who has entered the Dispensation of the Buddha desiring to realize Nibbana having seen the fear in the round of rebirths! Listen to the Four Resources prescribed by the Great Compassionate One for the maintenance of the life of a renunciant.

(1) “Piṇḍiyālopa bhojanaṃ nissāya pabbajjā. Tattha te yāvajīvaṃ ussāho karaṇīyo. Atireka lābho saṅghabhattaṃ. Uddesabhattaṃ, nimantaṇaṃ, salākabhattaṃ, pakkhikaṃ, uposathikaṃ, pāṭipadikaṃ.”

Friend! This going-forth is on account of food received by alms-gathering. Therein effort is to be made by you for the rest of your life. Extra allowances are: a meal for the entire Community of monks, a meal for a specific number of monks, an invitation, a meal given by tickets, a meal given on a day of the half-month, a meal given on an Uposatha day, and a meal given on the day after an Uposatha day; these should be accepted as extra allowances.

(2) “Paṃsukūla cīvaraṃ nissāya pabbajjā, tat‍tha te yāvajīvaṃ ussāho karaṇīyo. Atireka lābho khomaṃ kappāsikaṃ. Kosey‍yaṃ. Kambalaṃ. Sāṇaṃ. Bhaṅgaṃ.”

The going-forth is on account of rag-robes. Therein effort is to be made by you for the rest of your life. If, through the power of your previous merit, there should accrue to you extra allowances: robes made of linen, cotton, silk, wool, coarse hemp, or a mixture of these, they may be used as extra allowances.

(3) “Rukkhamūlasenāsanaṃ nissāya pabbajjā. Tat‍tha te yāvajīvaṃ ussāho karaṇīyo. Atireka lābho vihāro. Aḍḍhayogo. Pāsādo. Hammiyaṃ. Guhā”

The going-forth is on account of a lodging at the foot of a tree. Therein effort is to be made by you for the rest of your life, dwelling at the foot of a tree. If, through the power of your previous merit, there should accrue to you extra allowances: a monastery, a curved house, a long house, a mansion, or a cave, these also may be used.

(4) “Pūtimuttabhesajjaṃ nissāya pabbajjā. Tat‍tha te yāvajīvaṃ ussāho karaṇīyo. Atireka lābho sappi. Navanītaṃ telaṃ. Madhu. Phānitaṃ”

The going-forth is on account of fermented urine as medicine. Therein effort is to be made by you for the rest of your life to maintain yourself with fermented urine as medicine. If, through the power of your previous merit, there should accrue to you extra allowances: ghee, fresh butter, oil, honey, or molasses, these may be used as extra allowances.
