Matters for which one may go with a seven-day allowance.

It does not appear in the Monastic Procedures that one should go with a seven-day allowance for any reason such as pleasure trips, pilgrimage trips, visiting relatives, or visiting friends. “Dhamma savanaṭṭhāya animantitenā gantuṃ na vaṭṭati uddesa paripucchādīnaṃ atthāyāpi gantuṃ na labhati” In the Vinaya commentary, it is stated that one should not go without an invitation even for hearing the Dhamma, learning, or questioning. “Ācariyaṃ passissāmīti gantuṃ labhati” it is stated that one may go to see one's teacher.

Anujānāmi bhikkhave! sattannaṃ sattāhakaraṇīyena appahite’pi gantuṃ, pageva pahite. Bhikkhussa bhikkhuniyā sikkhamānāya sāmaṇerassa sāmaṇeriyā mātuyā ca pitussa ca. Anujānāmi bhikkhave imesaṃ sattannaṃ sattāhakaraṇīyena appahite’pi gantuṃ, pageva pahite. Sattāhaṃ sannivatto kātabbo.

Thus, the Tathagata has allowed that one may go with a seven-day allowance for these seven: a bhikkhu, a bhikkhunī, a probationer, a novice, a novice nun, one's mother, and one's father, even without an invitation.

“Anujānāmi bhikkhave! Saṅghakaraṇīyena gantuṃ sattāhaṃ sannivatto kātabbo” it has been declared that one may go for a Community-procedure and stay for a week, even without an invitation. A Community-procedure refers to work such as the renovation of monasteries, stupas, or monastic dwellings. This includes finding workers and materials for such tasks. Since every bhikkhu is included in the Community, going for matters concerning bhikkhus' personal dwellings is also considered going for a Community-procedure. For the work of others, apart from the individuals mentioned above, one should go with a seven-day allowance only if there is an invitation. In case of danger, leaving the place where one has entered the Rains-residence, even if it results in the breaking of the Rains-residence, does not incur an offense. Similarly, if one goes to see one's teacher and stays there as per the teacher's instruction, breaking the Rains-residence does not incur an offense.
