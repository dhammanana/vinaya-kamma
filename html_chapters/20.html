The Higher Penalty (Brahmadaṇḍa)

The Tathāgata prescribed the Higher Penalty for the bhikkhu named Channa, who behaved by reviling the bhikkhus and refusing to accept the exhortations and instructions of his fellow celibates. The Vinaya Commentary states: “Yo añño’pi bhikkhu mukharo hoti bhikkhuṃ aruttavacanehi ghaṭṭento khuṃsento vamhento viharati tassā’pi dātabbo” — meaning this penalty should also be imposed upon any other harsh-spoken bhikkhu who dwells harassing, disparaging, and scorning bhikkhus with improper speech. This penalty must be imposed by an Act of Proclamation (apalokana-kamma).

The Formal Act (Kammavācā).

Bhante (it‍thaṃ nāmo) bhikkhu mukharo, bhikkhu duruttavacanehi ghaṭṭento viharati. So bhikkhu yaṃ icchey‍ya taṃ vadey‍ya. Bhikkhūhi (it‍thaṃ nāmo) bhikkhu neva vattabbo, na ovaditabbo, na anusāsitabbo. Saṅghaṃ bhante pucchāmi (itthannāmassa) bhikkhuno b‍rahmadaṇḍassa dānaṃ. Ruccati saṅghassa.

Dutiyampi -pe- tatiyampi -pe-

After this act is performed, whatever that bhikkhu may say, the other bhikkhus shall say nothing to him. He shall not be exhorted nor instructed. If that bhikkhu later becomes disciplined, abandons his evil ways, and asks for forgiveness from the Community, the penalty shall be revoked by the Community.

The Formal Act for Revocation.

Bhante! bhikkhusaṅgho (asukassa) bhikkhuno b‍rahmadaṇḍaṃ adāsi. So bhikkhu sorato nivātavutti lajjidhammaṃ okkanto, hirottappe patiṭṭhito, paṭisaṅkhā āyatiṃ saṃvare tiṭṭhati. Saṅghaṃ bhante! pucchāmi tassa bhikkhuno b‍rahmadaṇḍassa paṭippassaddhiṃ. Ruccati saṅghassa.
