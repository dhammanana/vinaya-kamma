The Method of Conduct for Bhikkhus Fulfilling Penance.

There are numerous duties to be observed by bhikkhus fulfilling probation (parivāsa) and penance (mānatta). The breach of some of these results in the interruption of nights (ratticcheda) and an offense of wrong-doing (dukkaṭa). The breach of others results only in an offense of wrong-doing. Among these, the factors causing the interruption of nights must be most carefully considered. Even if the duties are fulfilled, if an interruption of nights occurs—even unknowingly—the days are not completed, and if rehabilitation (abbhāna) is performed, the bhikkhu does not attain purification. Therefore, one must take special care to avoid the interruption of nights.

“Tayo kho upāli! pārivāsikassa bhikkhuno ratticchedā sahavāso, vippavāso, anārocanā ime kho upāli! tayo pārivāsikassa bhikkhuno ratticchedā”

Thus, in the Cūḷavaggapāḷi, three factors are declared that cause the interruption of nights for a bhikkhu on probation: co-residence (sahavāsa), living apart (vippavāsa), and non-announcement (anārocanā). 'Co-residence' means sleeping under one roof in the same building with a bhikkhu of natural standing or with another bhikkhu fulfilling probation or penance. It should be noted that 'sleeping' does not strictly mean falling asleep. Whether one has fallen asleep or not, remaining in a sleeping posture is what is meant here by 'sleeping'. If one is sitting and leaning against a pillar or a wall, even if one falls asleep, it is not considered 'sleeping'. When two bhikkhus reside in one building, if one bhikkhu is sleeping while the other remains in a different posture, there is no interruption of nights. If both sleep at the same time, an interruption of nights occurs. If both are fulfilling probation, the interruption of nights occurs for both. If another bhikkhu enters and sleeps in the building where a bhikkhu fulfilling his duties is sleeping, without the latter's knowledge, an interruption of nights occurs for the one on probation; however, because he was unaware, there is no offense.

'Living apart' means passing the dawn in a place where there are no bhikkhus, separated from other bhikkhus. 'Non-announcement' means failing to announce one's status upon seeing a bhikkhu or hearing a bhikkhu's voice. If a bhikkhu on probation is on this side of a river and sees a bhikkhu on the other side, and is unable to announce his status to him, an interruption of nights occurs.

“Cattāro kho upāli! mānattacārikassa bhikkhuno ratticchedā, sahavāso, vippavāso, anārocanā, ūne gaṇe caraṇaṃ. Ime kho upāli! cattāro mānattacārikassa bhikkhuno ratticchedā”

Thus, four factors are declared as causes for the interruption of nights for a bhikkhu undergoing penance: co-residence, living apart, non-announcement, and practicing in an incomplete group. Practicing in an incomplete group means practicing the penance in a group of fewer than four bhikkhus.

During the period of fulfilling probation or penance, the bhikkhu must not act as a preceptor (upajjhāya) for higher ordination. He must not recite the formal act (kammavācā). If a situation arises where these must be done, he must first set aside (nikkhepa) the duty. He must not give dependence (nissaya). If there are pupils (antevāsika) who have taken dependence from him, he must tell those bhikkhus: "I am performing a disciplinary act. You must not perform duties for me. You must not ask me for permission to enter the village, etc." He must not be a preceptor for novices (sāmaṇera). If there are novices who have previously taken him as preceptor, he must tell them the same. If they continue to perform duties out of respect for their teacher even after being told, there is no offense in accepting. He must not fall into the same offense for which he undertook the duty, nor into any similar offense.

He must not act as the head of a monastery. On an Observance day (uposatha), he must not recite the Pātimokkha nor accept an invitation to recite it. He must not make accusations against other bhikkhus. Even if he is senior in ordination, he must not walk in front of bhikkhus of natural standing. He must not sit on a high seat. He must not accept invitations from donors and lead other bhikkhus. When traveling with the Community, he must walk alone, maintaining a distance of twelve cubits. When sitting in the dining hall or similar places, he must take the last seat. He must not sit on a seat while bhikkhus of natural standing are sitting on the ground. He must not sit on a high seat while bhikkhus of natural standing are on low seats. For residence in the monastery, he must take the most inferior lodging. When the Community assembles for the Observance (uposatha) or the Invitation (pavāraṇā), he must announce his duty. During that period, he must not fall again into an offense requiring probation or penance. He must not fall into any other such offense. If he falls into another grave offense (garukāpatti) while fulfilling the duty, he must fulfill the probation and penance again from the beginning. Details regarding this should be learned from the Cūḷavagga Pāḷi and the Vinaya Commentary.

If one fulfills the probation without setting it aside, there is no fault in not re-announcing to the bhikkhus to whom it has already been announced. If a visiting bhikkhu is encountered, the announcement must be made. If one sets aside the duty daily and undertakes it only during the night, the announcement must be made every day upon each undertaking. As for the penance (mānatta), even if it is not set aside, it must be announced to the Community every day.
