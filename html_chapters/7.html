Entering the Vassa

There are two types of entering the Vassa: the First Vassa and the Second Vassa. On the full moon day of Esala, which is the eighth Uposatha of the summer season, having observed the Uposatha, one should enter the First Vassa on the first day of the waning moon. On the full moon day of Nikini, which is the second Uposatha of the rainy season, having observed the Uposatha, one should enter the Second Vassa on the first day of the waning moon. For a bhikkhu who does not enter the Vassa on one of these two days, an offense of dukkata is incurred, according to this Buddha-enactment: “Na bhikkhave! Vassam na upagantabbam, yo na upagaccheyya apatti dukkatassa.”

On the day of entering the Vassa, having tidied the monastery where one will reside for the Vassa, in the evening, having offered flowers and lamps, paid homage to the Triple Gem, recited paritta, and transferred merit to the devas, one should enter the Vassa by saying “Imasmim vihare imam temasam vassam upemi” once, twice, or thrice.

If a bhikkhu residing in a certain monastery with the intention of "I will reside here for the Vassa" forgets to declare the Vassa by saying the phrase “Imasmim vihare imam temasam vassam upemi” due to being engaged in activities such as teaching Dhamma, the entering of the Vassa is still accomplished because the intention to enter the Vassa exists. If he resides without breaking the Vassa, there is no impediment for that bhikkhu to perform the Pavarana.

As it has been declared “Na bhikkhave! Asenasanikena vassam upa gantabbam. Yo upagaccheyya apatti dukkatassa,” one must enter the Vassa only after finding a dwelling with a roof and a door. If there is no dwelling for entering the Vassa, and no supporter to have one built, then one must find helpers and have a dwelling built. If helpers cannot be found, then one must build it oneself. One cannot avoid offense by not entering the Vassa on the pretext of not having a dwelling.
