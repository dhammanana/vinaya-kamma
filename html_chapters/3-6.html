Persons Ineligible for Higher Ordination

The thirteen persons—namely, the eleven types of persons such as the paṇḍaka mentioned above, the person who has committed a pārājika offense, and the person who has not completed twenty years of age—are ineligible for Higher Ordination. Even if the Act of Higher Ordination is performed, the Higher Ordination does not take effect for them. If those with other disqualifications are given Higher Ordination, the officiating Community of monks incurs an offense of wrong-doing (dukkaṭa). However, the Higher Ordination takes effect for them. All those who are designated as unfit for Going Forth (pabbajjā) are also unfit for Higher Ordination. Nevertheless, if those such as those with severed hands (hatthacchinna) and others are given Higher Ordination, the Higher Ordination takes effect for them. By that act, the officiating Community of monks and the teachers and preceptors incur an offense. Therefore, in the Monastic Procedures Commentary named Samantapāsādikā, it is stated: “Yesaṃ ca pabbajjā paṭikkhittā upasampadāpi tesaṃ paṭikkhittā va. Sace pana saṅgho upasampādeti sabbepi hatthacchinnādayo suvupasampannā, kārakasaṅgho pana ācariyupajjhāyā ca āpattito na muccati”.
