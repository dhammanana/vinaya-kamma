Monks Suitable to Be an Upajjhāya

Not all are suitable to become an Upajjhāya and give novice ordination and Higher Ordination to disciples. A bhikkhu who, without being suitable, becomes an Upajjhāya and gives novice ordination and Higher Ordination to disciples incurs an offence of dukkata.

“Na bhikkhave! Bālena abyattena upasampādetabbo. Anujānāmi bhikkhave! Byattena bhikkhunā paṭibalena dasavassena vā atireka dasavassena vā upasampādetuṃ” This is the word of the Buddha.

“Bhikkhus, Higher Ordination should not be given by a foolish, inexperienced bhikkhu. Bhikkhus, I allow Higher Ordination to be given by a bhikkhu who is experienced, capable, and has ten years or more than ten years [since his Higher Ordination],” this is its meaning.

Monks suitable to be an Upajjhāya are called ‘parisūpatthāyaka bahussuta’. The qualities of a parisūpatthāyaka bahussuta are as follows:

Being proficient in both the Bhikkhu and Bhikkhunī Vibhaṅgas of the Vinaya; knowing the discernment of what is a valid act and what is not; knowing the Vassakkhandhaka; having mastered either the Mūlapaṇṇāsaka from the Majjhima Nikāya, or the Mahāvagga from the Dīgha Nikāya, or the three lower sections or the Mahāvagga from the Saṃyutta Nikāya, or half of the Aṅguttara Nikāya, or the Dhammapada with its commentary or the Jātaka book from the Khuddaka Nikāya; and having ten years since Higher Ordination—these are the qualities of a parisūpatthāyaka bahussuta.

After Higher Ordination:

A newly ordained bhikkhu must reside with his Upajjhāya, associating with him, in the same place where the Upajjhāya resides, until five years have passed. A novice bhikkhu who resides separated from his Upajjhāya incurs an offence of dukkata with each dawn. An Upajjhāya who, having given Higher Ordination to others, then abandons them, also incurs an offence of dukkata due to breaking the observance. Unless one is a ‘nissaya muttaka bahussuta’, a bhikkhu must reside with his Upajjhāya or an ācariya for life.

Having memorized both Prātimokṣas; being proficient in four recitations (bāṇavara) from the Sutta Piṭaka; having practiced some method of discourse to teach those who come to him; having practiced three discourses for the purpose of anumodanā (rejoicing) on auspicious and inauspicious occasions; knowing about Monastic Procedures such as Uposatha and Pavāraṇā; having practiced some path of meditation; and having completed five years since Higher Ordination—these are the qualities of a nissaya muttaka bahussuta.
